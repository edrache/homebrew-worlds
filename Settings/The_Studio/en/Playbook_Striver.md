# THE STRIVER

*You were the best assistant anyone ever had. Now you're the youngest junior executive in the building. The gap between those two sentences is the rest of your career.*

**Damage Die:** d6
**HP:** 6 + CON

---

## Name

Pick a name:

**New Hollywood:** Quinn, Sloane, Avery, Tatum / Caden, Remy, Finn, Beckett
**Transplant Making It:** Priya, Leila, Zara, Mia / Kofi, Emre, Deon, Piers
**Ironic Industry Name:** Ida, Addie, Gilda, Lila / Capra, Wilder, Hawks, Sturges

## Look

Pick one from each row:

**Energy:** controlled precision with alarming depth beneath it | faster than everyone else in the room | dangerously competent for someone this age
**Age:** young enough to scare the executives | technically young, acting several years older | the youngest person in this meeting and everyone knows it
**Eyes:** sharp and recording everything | professional warmth over real assessment | briefly, nakedly ambitious, before she catches herself
**Build:** always in motion, even standing still | precise and economical | tired in ways that don't show yet
**Outfit:** business ready at all times, including weekends | thoughtfully assembled on this salary | whatever signals she belongs here
**Tell:** the pause before answering that means she already knows | completing your sentence correctly | loyalty you should not have relied on this heavily

## Background

Pick one.

---

**The Best Assistant in the Building**

You knew things before anyone told you. When you act on information you gathered through observation, proximity, or quiet diligence—without being told—take +1 forward.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you treated me differently than a typical assistant?
- Which of you knows something because I told you?
- Which of you didn't notice me until I stopped being an assistant?
- Which of you still asks me to get their coffee?

---

**The Ethical Tightrope**

You came in with principles. You've already bent one. When you refuse to cross a line that others are crossing, take +2 forward on the next move you make using your own judgment.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you knows where my line actually is?
- Which of you has asked me to cross it?
- Which of you was there when I first bent the rules?
- Which of you thinks I'll end up just like them?

---

**The Fast Learner**

You absorb pressure and convert it to efficiency. When something genuinely terrible happens and you stay calm, roll +CON. On a 10+, choose 2. On a 7-9, choose 1:
- The crisis timeline compresses
- You identify the one person who can actually fix this
- You avoid blame
- You learn who breaks under pressure

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you panicked and I covered for them?
- Which of you thought I was cold for not breaking down?
- Which of you has mistaken my efficiency for not caring?
- Which of you actually asked me how I was doing?

## Starting Moves

**The Protégé's Gambit**
When you execute a complex logistical task—scheduling, redirecting resources, solving a problem before anyone asks—roll +INT. On a 10+, it's done before anyone knew it was a problem. On a 7-9, it's done, but you've made one decision that wasn't yours to make.

**Systemic Optimization**
When you spend time working out how a system—a project, a schedule, a person's routine—actually operates, ask the GM one question about its weakest point.

**Idealism Intact**
As long as you haven't compromised your core values yet this session, take +1 ongoing to any move made in service of a genuinely good creative outcome.

**Organizational Sabotage**
When you redirect, delay, or quietly neutralize someone else's plan without them noticing, roll +INT. On a 10+, they don't notice. On a 7-9, they don't notice yet.

---

## Advances

- **The Jaded Turn:** Once per session, make a move as if you had no principles left. It's more effective. Nobody can call you on it yet.
- **Mentor's Successor:** When you are the most competent person in the room and there's no one above you, take +2 forward on all Discern Realities moves.
- **The Long Rope:** Once per session, someone gives you more autonomy than they intended. Use it before they notice.
- **Calendar Control:** You control one schedule per session completely. The person whose schedule it is doesn't find out until it's done.
- **Improved Stat:** Increase one stat by +1, to a max of +2.
- **Superior Stat** *(Requires: Improved Stat, at least one other advance)*: Increase one stat by +1, to a max of +3.

---

## Drives

- Do something genuinely important that you'll get no credit for.
- Stay yourself while doing everything that is asked of you.
- Call someone out for treating you like you still work for them.
- Choose between the right call and the safe call.
