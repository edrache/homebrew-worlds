*Problems love you. So do people. Unfortunately, these two facts are directly related.*

**Damage Die:** d6
**HP:** 6 + CON

## Background

Pick one.

---

**The Best Friend**

You've been best friends with someone powerful since before they were powerful. Loyalty is your currency and your blindspot. When you act to protect a specific named ally—declare them at character creation—take +1 forward.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you have I covered for without being asked?
- Which of you has watched me lie to protect someone who didn't deserve it?
- Which of you thinks I'm too loyal?
- Which of you has used that loyalty against me?

---

**The Talent Whisperer**

Difficult people become manageable around you. When you intervene to calm, distract, or redirect a talent in crisis, roll +CHA. On a 10+, the crisis passes and they like you more than before. On a 7-9, the crisis passes, but you've made a promise you'll need to keep.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you has seen me talk someone off a ledge—professional or literal?
- Which of you knows the worst thing I did to smooth something over?
- Which of you has benefited from my reputation with talent?
- Which of you doesn't trust me, and is right?

---

**The Studio Survivor**

You've outlasted three restructurings and four heads of production. When you Defy Danger using the fact that everyone in this building needs you for something, take +1 forward.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you has worked with me longer than anyone else here?
- Which of you tried to get me fired once?
- Which of you is the reason I'm still here?
- Which of you thinks I'm the most dangerous person in this building?

## Starting Moves

**Chaotic Neutral Enabler**
When you resolve a crisis through an unorthodox method, roll +CHA. On a 10+, it works, and only you know what you actually did. On a 7-9, it works, but choose one: you've created a secondary crisis, you now owe someone, or they saw exactly what you did.

**Popular with Talent**
When you reach out to a creative person—director, actor, writer—for a favor, roll +CHA. On a 10+, they do it, happily. On a 7-9, they do it, but mention it to three other people.

**Weasel Room**
When a situation deteriorates and you were involved in making it worse, you may exit cleanly once per session. No blame lands on you. You were never there.

**First In, First Out**
When you arrive somewhere early or leave somewhere smoothly before everything goes wrong, ask the GM what you saw that no one else did.

---

## Advances

- **Emotional Volatile:** Once per session, have a genuine emotional reaction so specific and unexpected that everyone in the room backs down.
- **The Favor Network:** Name any three people in the industry. They each owe you one. Ask the GM what.
- **Problem Generator, Problem Solver:** When you cause a new crisis while solving another one, gain +1 forward on the new crisis immediately.
- **The Pivot:** When you're caught in an obvious lie, roll +CHA. On a 10+, they believe the new version. On a 7-9, they half-believe it—but that's enough.
- **Improved Stat:** Increase one stat by +1, to a max of +2.
- **Superior Stat** *(Requires: Improved Stat, at least one other advance)*: Increase one stat by +1, to a max of +3.

---

## Drives

- Get someone out of a crisis of your own making.
- Be genuinely, selflessly helpful to one person who doesn't expect it.
- Go an entire session without creating a secondary problem.
- Decide, once, whether the loyalty is worth it.
