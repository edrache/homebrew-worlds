# THE TITAN

*You ran this town once. You will again, or die pretending you're not trying.*

**Damage Die:** d8
**HP:** 8 + CON

## Look

Pick one from each row:

**Presence:** entering every room like you still own it | effortlessly intimidating | deceptively warm until you're not
**Age:** old enough to have forgotten more than they know | wearing the decades like armor | carrying experience as a weapon
**Eyes:** evaluating everyone at all times | briefly, genuinely tender | the eyes of someone who has fired excellent people
**Build:** impeccably maintained | comfortably powerful | smaller than the room expects
**Outfit:** immaculate and expensive, always | casually wearing something that costs more than their salary | dressed down to make you underestimate her
**Tell:** unsettling stillness before speaking | the compliment that is also a threat | calling everyone "darling" at slightly different warmth levels

## Background

Pick one.

---

**The Ousted Head**

You built this studio and were removed by a CEO who didn't understand what you'd built. When you reveal institutional knowledge no one else could have, the room defers to your authority—even the people who replaced you.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you worked under me before?
- Which of you knew I was being pushed out and said nothing?
- Which of you voted to keep me?
- Which of you thinks I deserved it?

---

**The Reluctant Mentor**

You never wanted to teach. Teaching implied succession, which implied departure. When you give someone genuinely honest advice—the kind it costs you to share—take +1 forward on any action taken together with them this session.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you has been asking me for advice and getting only half of it?
- Which of you reminds me of myself in a way I find uncomfortable?
- Which of you do I secretly want to succeed?
- Which of you would I sacrifice to protect my own position?

---

**The Comeback Arc**

You have done this before and survived it. When you've already failed at something this session and attempt it again, roll +WIS instead of whichever stat applies.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you underestimated me?
- Which of you told a journalist I was finished?
- Which of you believed in me when I stopped believing in myself?
- Which of you is waiting for me to fail again?

---

## Starting Moves

**The Bitter Mentor**
When you share genuine industry intelligence with someone—real costs, real names, real risks—you may give them +2 forward. But: name what it costs you. Reputation, a relationship, or the admission that you need them.

**Mastery of Rooms**
When you walk into a professional space and take control of it, roll +WIS. On a 10+, choose 2. On a 7-9, choose 1:
- Everyone assumes you belong here
- No one contradicts you until you've finished speaking
- You identify the person actually in charge
- You spot the exit before you need it

**The Long Game**
When you sacrifice something today to position for something bigger, write down what you gave up. Ask the GM when it pays off.

**Meat Grinder Memory**
Once per session, you can tell someone exactly how a situation ends—based on having seen it before. You're right about the shape of it, even if the details differ.

**Industry Lore**
When you Discern Realities about studio politics, industry relationships, or the history of a project, ask one extra question.

---

## Advances

- **Absolute Authority:** Once per session, issue a directive. Anyone below CEO level follows it without question.
- **Old Loyalties:** Name any person in the industry. They owe you something. Ask the GM what.
- **Scar Tissue:** When you take psychological damage from a professional humiliation, it heals in the next scene—no roll needed.
- **The Exit Interview:** Once per campaign, declare you're done with someone professionally. They cannot directly hinder you again for the rest of the session.
- **Improved Stat:** Increase one stat by +1, to a max of +2.
- **Superior Stat** *(Requires: Improved Stat, at least one other advance)*: Increase one stat by +1, to a max of +3.

---

## Drives

- Take something back that was taken from you.
- Give genuinely good advice even when you'd rather not.
- Admit, to yourself, that you're addicted to this.
- Choose the protégé over your own position.
