*Cinema is a product. The product has an audience. The audience has data. You have the data. Checkmate, art.*

**HP:** 6 + CON

## Background

Pick one.

---

**The Convert**

You used to believe in cinema—you have a degree from a film school you no longer mention. When you invoke your buried artistic instincts to push back against pure commercialism, take +1 forward and give your opponent a reason to like you.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you knows about my film school past?
- Which of you has seen me genuinely moved by a film?
- Which of you thinks I'm a sellout?
- Which of you has asked me what I actually like?

---

**The Platform Native**

You speak the language of every tech platform better than anyone in this building. When you translate a studio problem into digital strategy, the conversation either accelerates or derails—you choose which.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you learned something from me they'd rather not have known?
- Which of you has asked me to run numbers on something personal?
- Which of you thinks I'm a fad?
- Which of you has actually listened to what the data said?

---

**The Reality Check**

Everyone here needs someone willing to say the hard thing. When you deliver a genuinely devastating assessment of a project—correct, brutal, and helpful—take +2 to your next Parley move in this scene.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you needed to hear what I said?
- Which of you hated me for a week after I said it?
- Which of you came back and admitted I was right?
- Which of you is still angry?

## Starting Moves

**The Bottom Line**
When you present data, engagement metrics, or market analysis to support a position, roll +INT. On a 10+, the argument is won—it takes the CEO to override you. On a 7-9, the argument is won, but someone accuses you of missing the point entirely.

**TED Talk Mode**
When you explain a complex problem using jargon, frameworks, and total confidence, roll +CHA. On a 10+, everyone believes it applies to the situation at hand. On a 7-9, the convincing people are convinced, and the unconvinced are more so.

**Audience Research**
When you spend time analyzing someone's patterns—professional or personal—ask the GM one question about what they actually want versus what they say they want.

**The Veto**
Once per session, veto any creative decision on the grounds of "it won't track." This forces a roll: the creator rolls +CHA vs. your +INT. Whoever wins takes the room.

## Advances

- **Disruption:** Once per session, reframe a creative failure as a strategic opportunity. Everyone in the room gains +1 forward on the new direction.
- **The Pivot:** When a project tanks, you are never associated with it—you saw this coming, you said so, it's all in the email.
- **Quantified Humanity:** When you apply data-driven thinking to a genuinely human problem and it actually works, take +2 forward on all subsequent moves this session.
- **Slap:** Once per session, declare something "doesn't slap." That project is dead. No one can revive it until next session.
- **Improved Stat:** Increase one stat by +1, to a max of +2.
- **Superior Stat** *(Requires: Improved Stat, at least one other advance)*: Increase one stat by +1, to a max of +3.

---

## Drives

- Make a genuinely human creative decision and own it.
- Tell someone the truth about the numbers even when the truth is good.
- Defend something that doesn't have data behind it.
- Admit, to yourself, that you miss the movie you thought you'd make.
