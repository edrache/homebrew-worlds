# THE CINEPHILE

*You know every Kubrick cut, every Scorsese tracking shot, every Tarkovsky sequence that made you weep at twenty-two. You are here to make movies that matter. You will probably approve a Kool-Aid Man biopic by Thursday.*

**Damage Die:** d4
**HP:** 6 + CON

## Background

Pick one.

---

**The Critic's Apprentice**

You spent years assistant-editing for a revered, deeply unemployed film critic. You can quote Pauline Kael in a budget meeting. When you cite film history to make an artistic argument in a crisis, take +1 forward.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you thinks my taste is genuinely good?
- Which of you saw me approve a sequel and said nothing?
- Which of you has heard me say "this one's for the art" and watched it bomb?
- Which of you is responsible for the first time I compromised?

---

**The Filmmaker Who Pivoted**

You made one brilliant, deeply unprofitable film and have been explaining why that qualifies you for this role ever since. When you invoke your one great film to earn someone's trust or cooperation, roll +CHA. On a 10+, it works. On a 7-9, it works, but someone mentions the box office number.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you has actually seen my film?
- Which of you thinks I sold out?
- Which of you keeps asking when my next one is coming?
- Which of you told the CEO about my Rotten Tomatoes score?

---

**The True Believer**

You genuinely think movies can change lives. This makes you excellent at your job and completely vulnerable to people who know it. When you articulate why a specific film matters—to a room, to a director, to anyone—take +2 forward to your next Likability roll.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you still believes what I believe?
- Which of you thinks my idealism is dangerous?
- Which of you has used my passion to get what they wanted?
- Which of you envies me for it?

---

**The Prodigy Overextended**

You were promoted three years faster than you should have been. The instincts are excellent; the experience is not. When you admit you don't know something and ask for help, the person you ask gives you a genuine answer—no games.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you trained me without realizing it?
- Which of you is jealous of my title?
- Which of you covered for a mistake I don't know I made?
- Which of you thinks I'll be fired within the year?

## Starting Moves

**The Likability Trap**
You carry a Likability resource (starts at 3). When you spend 1 Likability, a talent, director, or difficult executive does what you want—no roll required. If Likability reaches 0, you are a spineless suit in everyone's eyes until you earn it back.

**Encyclopedic**
When you name-drop a director, film, or movement to support your argument, roll +INT. On a 10+, no one can challenge your cultural authority on this. On a 7-9, it lands, but someone more respected contradicts you later.

**Earn It Back**
When you stand firm against an artistic compromise and it costs you something real, regain 1 Likability.

**Gut Feeling**
Once per session, greenlight something purely on instinct. Tell the GM what kind of film it is. Ask them one question about what happens next.

**Take the Note**
When a talent or director gives you humiliating, unsolicited feedback and you absorb it without flinching, take +2 forward.

## Advances

- **The Auteur's Ally:** When you champion a risky project, on a 7+ the creative team gains +1 ongoing for the production.
- **Double Back:** Once per session, reverse a decision you've already made. No one questions it.
- **Legendary Likability:** Your Likability maximum increases to 5.
- **No Notes:** Once per session, refuse any creative note from below the CEO level. They accept it—this time.
- **Name the Film:** When you're uncertain, name the film that prepared you for this. Take +1 forward and shrug off the next social setback.
- **Improved Stat:** Increase one stat by +1, to a max of +2.
- **Superior Stat** *(Requires: Improved Stat, at least one other advance)*: Increase one stat by +1, to a max of +3.

## Drives

- Greenlight a film that has no business getting made and make it work.
- Stand between a great director and someone trying to ruin their vision.
- Get through an entire meeting without referencing Kubrick.
- Admit you were wrong to someone you've genuinely disappointed.
