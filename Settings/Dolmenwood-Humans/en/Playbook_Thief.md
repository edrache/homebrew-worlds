# THE THIEF

*You have never once in your life walked through a door someone else opened for you. You learned early that everything worth having is locked, and you learned shortly after how to get around that.*

**Damage Die:** d4
**HP:** 4 + CON

## Background

Pick one.

---

**Thieves' Guild Member**

You operate under the auspices of Bagsley Corundum's guild, or you did until recently. You have contacts, drop points, and the knowledge of a professional. When you seek resources, fences, or safe houses through Guild channels, take +1 forward.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you knows the Guild — and how much of what they know is accurate?
- Which of you I trust with the fact that I still have obligations to them?
- Which of you have I recruited for something unofficial?
- Which of you I'm not sure the Guild knows I'm travelling with?

---

**Tomb Delver**

You specialise in places with traps, locks, and things that haven't been disturbed in a long time. When you investigate an old, sealed, or constructed structure for hidden mechanisms, take +1 forward and the GM must tell you if anything dangerous has been triggered or disturbed recently.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you I've saved from a trap that would have killed them?
- Which of you came on a job with me before this?
- Which of you has something from a delve that I found first?
- Which of you trusts my read of an old building?

---

**Pickpocket and Charmer**

You work the crowds and the social spaces. You know how to take what you want off someone while they're smiling at you. When you use sleight of hand, misdirection, or social engineering to take something or move through a space unnoticed, take +1 forward.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you caught me once and chose to say nothing?
- Which of you I've taken something from that I've since given back?
- Which of you I genuinely like, and so I don't work them?
- Which of you I've covered for in a social situation?

---

**Desperate Noble**

You come from money — or you did. Debts, scandal, or simple failure brought you here. You understand how wealth works and how to move among those who have it. When you navigate noble or merchant society while concealing your current circumstances, roll +CHA instead of +DEX for Defy Danger.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you knows my real name?
- Which of you I've used my old connections for, at some cost to my dignity?
- Which of you has asked me why I went this way instead of another?
- Which of you I'm trying not to need?

## Starting Moves

**Backstab**
When you attack an enemy who is unaware of you or distracted, deal +1d6 damage. If you attack from hiding, you are not automatically revealed.

**Climb**
You can scale sheer surfaces, ropes, and rough stone with no roll under ordinary conditions. In extreme conditions (slippery, vertical, hostile), roll +DEX.

**Disarm Traps**
When you examine a mechanism you believe is trapped, roll +DEX. On a 10+, you disarm it cleanly. On a 7-9, you disarm it but choose one: it's noisy, it partially triggers, or you leave it half-armed.

**Pick Locks**
With thieves' tools and time, you can open most locks without a roll. In time pressure or without tools, roll +DEX.

**Stealth**
When you move without being observed, take +1 forward. You can hide in shadows, reduce your noise to near-silence, and follow a target at distance without being noticed on a 10+.

**Decipher Script**
When you examine coded writing, arcane notation, or an unfamiliar language, roll +INT. On a 10+, you understand it. On a 7-9, you understand the gist and the GM tells you what you get wrong.

## Advances

- **Second Strike:** When you Backstab, you may immediately make a second attack if the first kills your target.
- **Smooth Exit:** When things go wrong, you can slip out of a situation — a building, a fight, a conversation — without a roll, once per scene.
- **Contacts:** You have established contacts in two locations in Dolmenwood — fences, safe houses, information brokers. They owe you something, or you owe them.
- **Legerdemain:** You can palm, swap, or produce small objects without anyone noticing, even under scrutiny.
- **Forgery:** Given an hour and reference material, you can produce a convincing copy of a document, signature, or official seal. The GM determines how good it needs to be to succeed.
- **Improved Stat:** Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +2.
- **Superior Stat** *(Requires: Improved Stat, at least one other advance)*: Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +3.

## Drives

- Take something from someone who deserved to lose it.
- Get into a place everyone said was impossible to enter.
- Pull off a job cleanly — no mess, no trail, no one knows it was you.
- Help someone who had no reason to expect help from your kind.
