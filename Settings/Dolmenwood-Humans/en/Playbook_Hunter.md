# THE HUNTER

*You know the Wood the way others know their own hands. You move through it quietly, you read it honestly, and you do not pretend it is safe.*

**Damage Die:** d8
**HP:** 8 + CON

## Background

Pick one.

---

**Bonded Hunter**

You have formed a genuine bond with an animal companion — a hound, a hawk, a badger, a pony, or something else the Wood offers. The bond is mutual and real. When you and your companion act together, take +1 forward.

Your companion: describe them and give them a name. They have 3 HP, one useful quality (fast, fierce, stealthy, aware), and they communicate needs clearly. When your companion is harmed, mark XP.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you has won my companion's trust?
- Which of you was unsettled by my companion at first?
- Which of you has helped care for them when I couldn't?
- Which of you my companion watches with particular attention?

---

**Game Warden**

You patrol a specific stretch of the Wood — you know its trails, its creatures, its moods. When you enter an area you know or one that resembles it, ask the GM: what creature in this area is most dangerous right now? The GM answers honestly.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you wandered into my territory before we met properly?
- Which of you knows the Wood well enough that I respect their experience?
- Which of you have I warned about something they ignored?
- Which of you I've decided to trust with places I don't usually share?

---

**Survivor of the Deep Wood**

You went too far in, once — farther than the trails reach — and you came back. You carry what that taught you. When you navigate or survive in genuinely wild, trackless terrain, take +1 forward.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you found me on the edge of the Wood when I returned?
- Which of you has asked what I saw out there?
- Which of you can tell, just by looking at me, that I came back different?
- Which of you I'm glad wasn't with me?

---

**Creature Scholar**

You have spent more time studying what lives in the Wood than fighting it, when possible. When you observe a creature before engaging — taking even a moment to read its behaviour — ask the GM one question about it. The GM answers honestly.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you has let me talk them out of a fight with something that didn't need to die?
- Which of you has asked what something was — and gotten a real answer?
- Which of you was surprised I knew so much?
- Which of you has seen me wrong about a creature in a costly way?

## Starting Moves

**Alertness**
You are never surprised. When something attacks from ambush or moves without warning, you act first regardless.

**Track**
When you study signs of passage — footprints, broken branches, disturbed earth — roll +WIS. On a 10+, you follow the trail without losing it and learn one thing about what left it. On a 7-9, you follow it but the GM chooses one: it took longer than expected, it led somewhere dangerous, or you're noticed.

**Wayfinding**
When your group becomes lost, there is a 4-in-6 chance you find the way again without a roll. On a failure, you can still roll +WIS.

**Missile Mastery**
When you attack with a bow or sling, deal +1 damage. At range, you take no penalty for difficult terrain or partial cover.

**Survival**
You can forage, make shelter, find water, and keep a group alive in the Wood without a roll under normal conditions. In unusual or hostile conditions, take +1 forward.

**Read the Creature**
When a creature behaves strangely — hesitates, circles, won't attack — you understand why. Ask the GM one question about what it wants or fears. The GM answers honestly.

## Advances

- **Second Animal Companion:** Bond with a second animal. Your first companion grows in loyalty — gain a second quality for them.
- **Deadly Aim:** Once per combat, before you shoot, declare you are aiming. Your attack deals maximum damage.
- **Quarry:** Declare one creature as quarry. Against it, all your rolls take +1 forward until it is dead or fled.
- **Forest Ghost:** When you move through wild terrain without alerting others, take +1 forward. You may hide in plain sight of creatures whose attention you haven't drawn.
- **First Aid, Field-Dressed:** When you treat a wound in the field with a knife and what the Wood provides, heal 1d8 HP to one character.
- **Improved Stat:** Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +2.
- **Superior Stat** *(Requires: Improved Stat, at least one other advance)*: Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +3.

---

## Drives

- Keep your companion alive when everything is trying to kill them.
- Lead someone through the Wood who couldn't have survived it alone.
- Find and face the creature that has been following your group.
- Learn one new truth about something that lives in the deep Wood.
