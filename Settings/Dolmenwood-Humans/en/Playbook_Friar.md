# THE FRIAR

*You go where the Church doesn't. You carry a staff, a prayer, and a frying pan if that's what the day calls for. You have decided that being useful matters more than being respected.*

**Damage Die:** d4
**HP:** 4 + CON

## Background

Pick one.

---

**Monastery-Trained**

You were educated at the Refuge of St Keye or one of the fallen abbeys. You know herb-lore, scripture, and how to keep a wound from killing someone. When you provide medical care or herbal treatment, take +1 forward.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you came to me with something that needed treating?
- Which of you has asked about my monastery and gotten a sad answer?
- Which of you thinks I'm wasted outside the Church walls?
- Which of you have I taught something small but useful?

---

**Wandering Preacher**

You took to the roads before most friars do. You know what people in small hamlets and lonely crossroads actually need — and it's rarely doctrine. When you offer comfort, counsel, or practical aid to a commoner, they tell you something true about the place you're in.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you I met on the road when we were both going nowhere?
- Which of you thinks the Church should do more of what I do and less of what it does?
- Which of you has asked for my blessing and gotten it without judgement?
- Which of you has come to me with something too heavy to carry alone?

---

**Plague Friar**

You served in a village or district during sickness and came out the other side. You are not afraid of infection, death, or bodies. When you Defy Danger against disease, poison, or the aftermath of violence, take +1 forward.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you knows what I saw in that village?
- Which of you I helped when they were sick or hurt and they almost didn't make it?
- Which of you is quietly relieved that I am calm about these things?
- Which of you have I convinced not to give up?

---

**Saint's Devotee**

You are devoted to a specific saint — their area of patronage shapes your prayers and your path. When you invoke your saint in a situation relevant to their domain, take +1 forward and the GM must tell you something useful.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you has heard of my saint?
- Which of you have I brought the saint's protection to, whether they asked for it or not?
- Which of you thinks my devotion is superstition?
- Which of you has seen something that made them reconsider?

---

## Starting Moves

**Herbalism**
When you forage for herbs, fungi, or healing plants in wild terrain, roll +WIS. On a 10+, you find everything you need and learn one additional useful property of something growing nearby. On a 7-9, you find what you need but it takes longer or you expose yourself to something unpleasant.

**Holy Magic**
Beginning at Level 1, you may pray for spells each day, even without formal ordination. Your prayers are simple but sincere. The saints hear you.

**Armour of Faith**
The One True God shields those who do the work. You gain a bonus to Armour Class equal to your Level divided by 3 (round up), even without armour.

**Culinary Implements**
In a pinch, you can fight with a frying pan, ham shank, or cured sausage as a melee weapon (d4 damage). No one sees it coming. You take +1 forward the first time you do this in a scene.

**Tend the Wounded**
When you have supplies and spend a moment of calm, you may heal 1d6 HP to one creature. In the field, this requires herbs or bandages. At camp, it heals to full.

**Turn the Undead**
When you present your holy symbol and call on your saint against the undead, roll +WIS. On a 10+, 1d4 undead flee or are stunned. On a 7-9, they hesitate — the GM chooses one complication.

## Advances

- **Greater Herbalism:** When you prepare a specific treatment in advance (a poultice, a draught, a cure), it takes effect instantly when applied rather than requiring a roll.
- **Serene Under Pressure:** When a situation becomes violent or frightening and you remain calm, take +1 forward to your next action.
- **The Saint Provides:** Once per session, find exactly what you need — a specific herb, a small tool, a coin — in a pocket you didn't know you'd packed.
- **Preaching Voice:** When you speak truth plainly and without ornament to a crowd or group, they listen. No roll required for them to hear you out; they may still disagree.
- **Healing Touch:** When you lay hands on someone who is dying or has been brought to 0 HP, they stabilise automatically. No roll, no conditions.
- **Improved Stat:** Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +2.
- **Superior Stat** *(Requires: Improved Stat, at least one other advance)*: Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +3.

## Drives

- Tend to someone who had no reason to expect help.
- Speak plainly to someone powerful about what they are doing wrong.
- Find and use a plant no one else would have known to look for.
- Carry a burden for someone who can't carry it alone.
