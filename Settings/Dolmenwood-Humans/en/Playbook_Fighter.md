## Background

Pick one.

---

**Ducal Soldier**

You served in Castle Brackenwold's garrison. You know formations, orders, and when to stop asking questions. When you fight alongside an ally who is following a clear plan, you both take +1 forward.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you knows my rank — or the rank I used to hold?
- Which of you fought beside me before we ended up here?
- Which of you has asked why I left the garrison?
- Which of you I would still take an order from?

---

**House Guard**

You swore to a noble house and did the work that kept them breathing. You know what it costs to protect the powerful. When you Defend someone weaker than the threat they're facing, hold +1 Readiness.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you knows the house I served — and what happened to them?
- Which of you has needed protecting in a way that reminded me of my old work?
- Which of you thinks loyalty to a house is a weakness?
- Which of you would I put first in a fight, before any other consideration?

---

**Mercenary**

You sold your sword and took what came with it. No one owns your allegiance and you prefer it that way. When you negotiate payment before taking a dangerous job, take +1 forward to the first roll you make doing it.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you hired me — or knew someone who did?
- Which of you thinks I can be bought off the job?
- Which of you have I refused a job from, and why?
- Which of you I'd work for free, though I haven't said so?

---

**War Veteran**

You came back from a war no one here remembers. You carry techniques from that place. When you Defy Danger in active combat — under fire, in chaos, outnumbered — take +1 forward.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you knows what war I served in?
- Which of you has asked me about it and I answered honestly?
- Which of you saw me freeze once, for just a moment?
- Which of you I wouldn't want to see fight the same way I do?

## Starting Moves

**Combat Talent**
At Level 1, choose or roll one Combat Talent:
1. **Battle Rage:** When you enter a berserk fury, take +2 to Attack and Damage but -4 to armour and you cannot flee until combat ends.
2. **Cleave:** When you strike a killing blow in melee with multiple foes nearby, make an immediate second attack at -2.
3. **Defender:** While you are engaged in melee, enemies attacking others nearby are at -2.
4. **Slayer:** Choose a type of creature. Against them, your attacks deal +1d4 extra damage.

*(Gain additional Combat Talents at Levels 6, 10, and 14.)*

**Front Line**
When you put yourself between the enemy and a weaker ally, you draw their attention — enemies attack you first unless they have a specific reason not to.

**Weapon Mastery**
You are proficient with any weapon you can lift. When you fight with a weapon suited to the situation — right range, right weight, right moment — take +1 forward.

**Endure**
When you take damage that would take you to 0 HP, roll +CON. On a 10+, you stay conscious with 1 HP. On a 7-9, you stay conscious but the GM chooses one complication: you're exposed, disarmed, or staggered.

**Hold Ground**
When you stand your ground and refuse to retreat, enemies must test your resolve — roll +STR. On a 10+, they hesitate and you choose where the engagement happens. On a 7-9, they press but you take +1 armour against the first hit.

**Arsenal**
You begin each session with one weapon option the fiction supports — from your pack, a contact, or a stash you made earlier.

## Advances

- **New Combat Talent:** Gain an additional Combat Talent from the list.
- **Momentum:** When you deal damage that kills an enemy, take +1 forward.
- **Iron Constitution:** Your HP maximum increases by 4.
- **Tactical Eye:** When you assess an enemy's fighting style for a moment before engaging, ask the GM one question about their weaknesses. The GM answers honestly.
- **Press the Advantage:** When an ally creates an opening — by flanking, distracting, or wounding an enemy — you may move in and attack without provoking a response.
- **Improved Stat:** Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +2.
- **Superior Stat** *(Requires: Improved Stat, at least one other advance)*: Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +3.

---

## Drives

- End a fight cleanly before it spreads.
- Protect someone who couldn't have protected themselves.
- Test yourself against something stronger than you've faced before.
- Get paid what you're actually worth.
