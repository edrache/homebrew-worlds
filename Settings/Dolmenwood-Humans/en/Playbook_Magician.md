*You have read things no one recommended you read, asked questions the Institute preferred you not ask, and traded a great deal of sleep for a great deal of knowledge. You think it was worth it.*

## Background

Pick one.

---

**Royal Institute Graduate**

You were trained at the Institute in Castle Brackenwold — the only officially sanctioned magical college in Dolmenwood. You know proper theory, institutional politics, and where the Guild's interests end and yours begin. When you consult your formal training on an arcane question, take +1 forward.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you has heard of the Royal Institute?
- Which of you is suspicious of my formal credentials?
- Which of you I've impressed more than the Institute prepared me to?
- Which of you has asked me something the curriculum didn't cover?

---

**Self-Taught Eccentric**

You had no master and no curriculum. Your magic is strange, effective, and occasionally alarming to other practitioners. When you improvise a magical solution no trained magician would attempt, take +1 forward.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you was present for something I did that I still can't fully explain?
- Which of you thinks proper training would have made me dangerous in the wrong way?
- Which of you has caught me testing something on myself?
- Which of you trusts my methods despite themselves?

---

**Apprentice Who Outlasted Their Master**

Your master died, vanished, or became impossible to apprentice under. You know their techniques better than anyone alive now, and you carry their unfinished work. When you work on research your master began, ask the GM one question they would have known. The GM answers honestly.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you knows what happened to my master?
- Which of you I've shown my master's work to?
- Which of you thinks I'm carrying something I should put down?
- Which of you has helped me find a piece my master was looking for?

---

**Drune-Adjacent Scholar**

You have studied stolen Drune spells — the Wood's hidden cult magic — without becoming one of them. It makes your range unusual. When you identify or interact with Drune magic, take +1 forward and ask the GM one question. The GM answers honestly.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you knows the risk I took learning what I know?
- Which of you is unsure where my research ends and heresy begins?
- Which of you have I helped with something no other magician could have?
- Which of you has warned me about the path I'm on?

## Starting Moves

**Arcane Magic**
You memorise spells from your spellbook each day and cast them as written. At Level 1, you may memorise one Rank 1 spell per day. Memorised spells are expended on casting. Your spellbook is your foundation — without it, you must improvise.

**Starting Spell Book**
You begin with one spellbook and the spells it contains, rolled or chosen with the GM. This book may be inherited, stolen, bought, or copied under a mentor's eye.

**Detect Magic**
When you concentrate on a person, object, or location, you sense the presence and rough nature of magical effects. The GM tells you whether magic is present and what school it resembles.

**Magic Items**
You can identify and use magic items restricted to arcane spell-casters — wands, scrolls, enchanted implements. Other classes cannot.

**Esoteric Lore**
When you Spout Lore about arcane magic, magical creatures, or the history of sorcery in Dolmenwood, take +1 forward. You have read more than is good for you.

**Methodical Research**
When you spend meaningful time studying a specific magical problem — at least a watch — ask the GM two questions about it. The GM answers both honestly.

## Advances

- **New Spells:** Add 1d4 new spells to your spellbook from research, trade, or discovery.
- **Arcane Recovery:** Once per day, recover one expended Rank 1 spell without rest.
- **Wand Mastery:** When you use a magic wand, treat its effects as if you were one Level higher.
- **Staff Focus:** Your staff counts as a magic weapon. You deal d6 damage with it instead of d4.
- **Forbidden Spell:** Learn one spell outside your normal school — stolen, bargained for, or reconstructed from ruins.
- **Improved Stat:** Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +2.
- **Superior Stat** *(Requires: Improved Stat, at least one other advance)*: Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +3.

---

## Drives

- Learn a spell no living magician still knows.
- Solve a magical problem by doing it correctly, not cleverly.
- Recover or complete a piece of research that would otherwise be lost.
- Resist trading something important for something merely interesting.
