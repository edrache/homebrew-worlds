# THE BARD

*You carry songs older than anyone can remember. You have wandered every muddy road in the Wood and learned that a good story buys more than a sword ever could.*

**Damage Die:** d6
**HP:** 6 + CON

---

## Name

Pick a name:

**Noble-born:** Aldric, Severn, Rosmund, Cressida / Wulfrey, Pelham, Isolde, Merewyn
**Common Folk:** Smyde, Dapper, Brindle, Leechy / Tam, Rowse, Nance, Gwenny
**Itinerant:** Wick, Fallow, Crumble, Stave / Prill, Sorrow, Jestry, Hobbin
**Church Choir:** Constant, Devyn, Praise, Laudate / Hymnal, Wren, Vesper, Cantor

---

## Look

Pick one from each row:

**Heritage:** noble blood who chose the road | common-born wanderer | choir-trained voice | church dropout turned storyteller | ask the table
**Age:** young and full of borrowed tales | seasoned traveler with original ones | grizzled veteran of every tavern in the Wood
**Eyes:** bright and reading the room | warm but always calculating distance | sad in repose, electric when performing
**Build:** wiry and light on their feet | soft and commanding | lean with a traveler's endurance
**Clothing:** extravagant rags patched with fine fabric | simple coat with hidden pockets | performer's finery that's seen better roads
**Signature:** a lute slung across the back | a voice that carries without trying | a joke that arrives exactly when needed

## Background

Pick one.

---

**Bardic Guild Delegate**

You carry the seal of the Guild and the reputation that comes with it. When you invoke the Guild's name in a place where its influence is known, take +1 forward to Parley.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you has heard one of my songs before we met?
- Which of you is suspicious of what the Guild wants from me?
- Which of you asked me to remember something — a name, a face, a place?
- Which of you I wouldn't mind writing a song about?

---

**Survivor of the Wood**

You were lost in Dolmenwood for a season and came back changed. Your songs carry something true. When you perform a story about the Wood — its creatures, its roads, its strangeness — take +1 forward and the GM must answer one honest question about what you've described.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you found me on the road after I came back?
- Which of you can hear something different in my music?
- Which of you has asked me what happened in there, and gotten no real answer?
- Which of you believes I left something behind?

---

**Notorious Liar**

You have told so many stories that the truth and the invention are genuinely interchangeable. When you tell a plausible lie with conviction, the GM must treat it as credible until evidence surfaces.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you caught me in a lie and chose not to call it out?
- Which of you still doesn't know what's true about me?
- Which of you I've told the most honest things to, by accident?
- Which of you trusts me anyway?

---

**Road Preacher**

You sing to the downtrodden and the forgotten. When you perform for the poor, the sick, or the outcast, you may ask the GM one question about what they need. The GM answers honestly.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you has seen me give my last coin away?
- Which of you thinks I'm naive?
- Which of you have I helped when they didn't expect it?
- Which of you carries something I sang about?

---

## Starting Moves

**Counter-Charm**
When someone uses magic, enchantment, or supernatural persuasion against you or an ally nearby, roll +CHA. On a 10+, the effect is negated or reversed. On a 7-9, the effect is reduced — choose one: it's delayed, it only affects you, or it costs something small.

**Silver Tongue**
When you Parley using a story, song, flattery, or clever misdirection instead of threats or bribes, roll +CHA. On a 10+, they do what you want and don't feel manipulated. On a 7-9, they do it, but they want something small in return.

**Folklore**
When you hear about a creature, location, or figure from the Wood's history, you may ask the GM one honest question. You've heard this story before — somewhere, from someone.

**Ballad of the Road**
When you make camp and perform for the group, each ally who listens heals 1d4 HP and clears one negative condition.

**Charm of Misdirection**
When you distract a crowd, a guard, or a creature with performance, roll +CHA. On a 10+, all of them are distracted for as long as you keep performing plus one action after. On a 7-9, half of them are; choose which ones.

**Read the Room**
When you enter a new social space, ask the GM: who is in charge here, who resents them, and what does this room want right now? The GM answers all three honestly.

## Advances

- **Enchanting Refrain:** Once per scene, when you sing or play, one target is charmed and follows a simple suggestion as if they had agreed to it.
- **Stolen Lore:** When you decipher a script, inscription, or coded message, take +1 forward to using what you learn.
- **Voice of Command:** When you shout a single direct order in combat, one enemy hesitates — they cannot attack for one round unless the order is disobeyed.
- **Fast Friends:** When you meet someone new, you may ask the GM: what would make this person trust me? The GM answers honestly.
- **The Song Remembers:** Once per session, recall a specific piece of lore, name, or rumour that no one else could know — you heard it somewhere on the road.
- **Improved Stat:** Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +2.
- **Superior Stat** *(Requires: Improved Stat, at least one other advance)*: Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +3.

---

## Drives

- Collect a song no one else carries.
- Talk your way into somewhere you were never meant to be.
- Be remembered by someone who had no reason to remember you.
- Learn the truth behind a story you've been telling wrong.
