# THE KNIGHT

*You carry your liege's name on your shield and their honour on your back. You have decided that the code of chivalry is worth keeping even when no one is watching — especially then.*

**Damage Die:** d8
**HP:** 8 + CON

## Background

Pick one.

---

**House Harrowmoor — Steadfast Militant**

Lady Theatrice Harrowmoor's knights are known for discipline and resolve. You hold the line. When you Defend in the name of your house, take +1 Readiness and your allies take +1 forward on their first actions while you hold.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you knows the reputation of House Harrowmoor?
- Which of you thinks my discipline is too rigid?
- Which of you have I protected without making a show of it?
- Which of you would I invite to meet my liege?

---

**House Guillefer — Dreamy and Aloof**

House Guillefer's knights are known for their distance and their patience. Rulership rotates; your loyalty is to the house, not one lord. When you take the long view — declining to act rashly, waiting, observing — take +1 forward to the action you eventually choose.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you has been frustrated by my stillness?
- Which of you has benefited from my patience?
- Which of you I've surprised with a decisive act?
- Which of you I consider a peer without quite saying so?

---

**Knight-Errant**

You have left your liege's lands to prove your chivalric virtues through adventure. You answer to your honour code first, your liege second. When you act in a way that clearly demonstrates a chivalric virtue — courage, justice, mercy, loyalty — mark XP.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you I have treated with formal courtesy from the start?
- Which of you has tested my code and watched how I responded?
- Which of you thinks chivalry is something from stories?
- Which of you have I disappointed, and how do I make it right?

---

**Elevated from Below**

You were not born noble. Your knighthood was earned by deed. You know what the common folk actually face, and you hold your title with that knowledge. When you speak on behalf of the downtrodden to those with power, you may roll +CHA instead of +WIS for Parley.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you knows where I actually came from?
- Which of you respects me more for it?
- Which of you treats me as they would any noble?
- Which of you have I proved myself to, in a moment that counted?

## Starting Moves

**Horsemanship**
You fight as well mounted as on foot. When mounted, you gain +1 to attack rolls and +1 forward to Defy Danger involving riding or pursuit. Your warhorse is trained for combat.

**Monster Slayer**
Choose one type of dangerous creature (undead, giant beasts, chaotic creatures, or a specific monster type). Against them, your attacks deal +1d4 damage. You may change your designated quarry between sessions.

**Strength of Will**
When you face supernatural fear, beguiling magic, or coercion through intimidation, roll +CHA. On a 10+, you are unaffected. On a 7-9, you are shaken but act through it.

**Chivalric Code**
You are bound by code: honour your liege, defend the innocent, show mercy when it is earned, fight with courage. When you follow this code at genuine cost to yourself, mark XP. When you break it, your liege hears of it.

**Formal Challenge**
When you formally challenge an enemy — by name and title, in clear view — they must respond or be shamed. If they accept, they focus on you alone until the challenge is resolved.

**Noble Bearing**
When you enter a formal or courtly setting and act according to your rank, you are treated with appropriate status. Doors open; seats are offered; questions are answered before they are asked.

## Advances

- **Lance Charge:** When you charge on horseback and land a blow with your lance, deal double damage and the target is knocked down.
- **Fearless:** You are immune to mundane fear and take +1 forward against supernatural fear.
- **Liege's Favour:** Your liege grants you a formal boon — a name, a resource, a contact, a safe house — of significant value.
- **Knightly Reputation:** Your name is known in the courts of Dolmenwood. Nobles recognise you; common folk know the heraldry.
- **Rally:** Once per combat, when you call out to your allies with authority, they all clear Fear and take +1 forward.
- **Improved Stat:** Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +2.
- **Superior Stat** *(Requires: Improved Stat, at least one other advance)*: Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +3.

---

## Drives

- Act with honour when dishonour would be easier and no one would know.
- Defend someone who had no right to expect a knight's protection.
- Prove your liege's name means something in a place that has forgotten it.
- Face something that required courage — real courage, not duty.
