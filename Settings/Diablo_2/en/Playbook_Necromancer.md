## Background

Pick one.

---

**Priest of Rathma**

You were instructed to preserve the Balance, not comfort the living. When you name the cost of meddling with life and death, take +1 forward to make someone listen or reconsider.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you first mistook me for a grave-robber or cultist?
- Which of you has seen me show true reverence to the dead?
- Which of you believes the Balance is just an excuse for cruelty?
- Which of you has asked me to break my vows for someone they loved?

---

**Corpse-Field Adept**

You know how battlefields keep talking long after the screams end. When you examine a place of slaughter, ask the GM what died here badly, what still lingers, or what the dead want done.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you stood with me among too many bodies?
- Which of you refused to let me question the dead you buried?
- Which of you saw what followed me from that field?
- Which of you do I suspect will leave an interesting corpse?

---

**Bone Crafter**

You shape remains into tools, wards, and servants. Given time and suitable remains, you may fashion a small bone charm, needle, key, or marker with a useful eerie purpose.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you carries one of my carved protections?
- Which of you cannot bear the sound of my work?
- Which of you wants me to make something from a fallen enemy?
- Which of you worries I will treat your body as material?

---

**Spirit Listener**

The dead confide in you. The first time each session you accept guidance from a ghost, omen, or ancestral murmur, mark XP.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you heard me speaking when no living person was there?
- Which of you has a dead relative who keeps hovering near me?
- Which of you thinks the voices are lying to us?
- Which of you have I warned about the shadow on your heels?

## Starting Moves

**Raise the Dead**
When you animate a fresh corpse or pile of bones to serve briefly, roll +WIS. On a 10+, it obeys simple commands until it is destroyed or dawn comes. On a 7-9, as above, but choose one: it is fragile, it is hungry for violence, or it draws unwelcome attention.

**Bone Spear**
When you tear a razor shaft of bone from earth, wall, or corpse and launch it, deal your damage at `near`.

**Grave Sight**
When you look upon a place, object, or person touched by death, ask the GM one: what died here, what death follows this, or what is being kept from rest?

**Clay and Blood**
When you use herbs, blood, or grave-dust to bind a wound, heal an ally 1d6 damage, but leave a visible occult sign of your work.

**Command the Stillness**
When you face mindless undead and speak with the authority of the grave, roll +WIS. On a 10+, they pause, part, or turn from you. On a 7-9, they hesitate, but one hungers for your life-force.

**Harvest Essence**
When something unnatural dies near you, ask the GM what trace of power it leaves and take +1 forward when using it.

## Advances

- **Army of One Night:** When you raise dead from a battlefield or crypt, you may animate one extra servant.
- **Exploding Corpse:** When you turn a corpse into a weapon, deal 1 damage to everyone close enough to be caught in the ruin.
- **Bone Armor:** Once per fight, conjure a cage of ribs and shards to gain +1 armor.
- **Speak for the Dead:** When you question a corpse, it must answer one question clearly before the grave calls it back.
- **Cold Patience:** You never need to roll to keep your nerve in a tomb, crypt, or charnel place.
- **Improved Stat:** Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +2.
- **Superior Stat** *(Requires: Improved Stat, at least one other advance)*: Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +3.

---

## Drives

- Preserve the Balance where others would break it.
- Learn something useful from the dead.
- Use fear, omen, or the grave to control a situation.
- Claim a corpse, relic, or burial place of significance.
