## Background

Pick one.

---

**Knight of the Order**

You were taught ceremony, rank, and the art of inspiring others. When you openly invoke your office to command respect, roll +CHA. On a 10+, they hesitate or obey unless they have much to lose. On a 7-9, they demand proof, sacrifice, or immediate action.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you has seen me at my most devout?
- Which of you distrusts the church but not me?
- Which of you do I suspect needs absolution?
- Which of you once stood between me and disgrace?

---

**Relic-Bearer**

You carry a sanctified object of the old faith. Name it. When you present it against undead, demons, or profane sorcery, take +1 forward.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you knows what this relic cost my order?
- Which of you touched it without permission?
- Which of you believes it is more dangerous than holy?
- Which of you have I trusted to guard it once before?

---

**Templar of the Road**

You have marched from shrine to battlefield and back again. When the group makes camp in hostile ground, you may declare a defensible perimeter and ask the GM what threatens it first.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you kept watch beside me through a terrible night?
- Which of you thinks I am too rigid for what lies ahead?
- Which of you have I already warned about corruption?
- Which of you did I pull from the edge of death?

---

**Penitent Crusader**

You fight because once you failed. The first time each session you place yourself in danger to protect another from the evil you fear most, mark XP.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you knows what failure I still cannot name?
- Which of you thinks I seek death more than redemption?
- Which of you reminds me of someone I could not save?
- Which of you has asked me for forgiveness I cannot yet give?

## Starting Moves

**Holy Shield**
When you raise shield and prayer together against a visible threat, roll +CON. On a 10+, choose 2. On a 7-9, choose 1.
- You or an ally behind you avoids the worst of it.
- The foe is checked, staggered, or driven back.
- Your shield blazes with holy light until the danger passes.

**Smite Evil**
When you strike a demon, undead thing, or servant of corruption in melee, your attack gains `forceful`.

**Aura of Resolve**
When you speak a vow aloud in the face of terror, choose one ally who takes +1 forward to stand firm or press on.

**Lay On Hands**
When you press your hand to a living creature and pray for healing, roll +WIS. On a 10+, heal 1d8 damage or purge a lesser corruption. On a 7-9, as above, but you take the pain, exhaustion, or stain into yourself.

**Consecrated Ground**
When you spend a few moments with salt, oil, ash, or prayer to bless a place, minor undead and lesser demons cannot cross it without first revealing themselves.

**Hammer of Conviction**
When you name a wicked deed your foe is guilty of before you attack, take +1 forward if the accusation is true.

---

## Advances

- **Blinding Radiance:** When you reveal holy light in darkness, nearby lesser horrors recoil.
- **Unbroken Vow:** When you hold a door, bridge, gate, or threshold alone, take +1 armor.
- **Judgment:** Once per session, ask the GM which foe here is most damned and why.
- **Shield-Brother:** When you defend the same ally twice in a fight, mark XP.
- **Exorcist's Rite:** You may treat possession, curses, and unclean influences as physical foes for the purpose of driving them out.
- **Improved Stat:** Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +2.
- **Superior Stat** *(Requires: Improved Stat, at least one other advance)*: Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +3.

---

## Drives

- Put yourself between evil and the helpless.
- Demand truth, confession, or repentance.
- Preserve a sacred place, relic, or vow.
- Face corruption without compromise.
