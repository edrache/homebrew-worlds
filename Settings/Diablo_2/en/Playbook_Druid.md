## Background

Pick one.

---

**Keeper of the Old Ways**

You learned the ancient pacts of fire, beast, and standing stone. When you invoke forgotten custom to calm spirits, villagers, or wild things, take advantage.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you mocked my rites before seeing them work?
- Which of you has shared a fire and an honest silence with me?
- Which of you carries iron, greed, or blight the land dislikes?
- Which of you have I warned not to cut what should remain living?

---

**Wolf Brother**

You run more easily with beasts than with crowds. When you track by scent, sign, or animal instinct, take +1 forward.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you was there when I ran on four legs through the night?
- Which of you earned the trust of my companions in fur or feather?
- Which of you panics when the wild gets too close?
- Which of you did I choose not to leave behind in the forest?

---

**Storm Caller**

You have stood on a cliff and answered thunder with your own voice. When violent weather is already present, your elemental workings gain `forceful`.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you first saw lightning answer my anger?
- Which of you believes storms follow me for a reason?
- Which of you still flinches when my temper rises?
- Which of you asked me to bring ruin on your enemy?

---

**Last of a Circle**

Your grove, cairn, or stone ring is gone. The first time each session you defend a natural place from corruption, desecration, or needless ruin, mark XP.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you knows the name of the place I failed to save?
- Which of you helped bury one of my circle?
- Which of you thinks grief drives me more than wisdom?
- Which of you has promised to help me restore what was lost?

## Starting Moves

**Shapeshift**
When you call on the old wild and take the form of a known beast, roll +WIS. On a 10+, hold 3. On a 7-9, hold 2. Spend hold, 1 for 1, to make a move the beast would naturally make.

**Cyclone Voice**
When you command wind, rain, or thunder to hinder foes, roll +WIS. On a 10+, choose 2. On a 7-9, choose 1.
- They are thrown off balance.
- The weather covers an escape or advance.
- Something fragile or evil is battered apart.

**Wild Empathy**
When you calm, feed, or face an animal without force, it will not attack unless badly provoked.

**Summon the Pack**
When you call nearby beasts, spirits of fang, or hunting birds for aid, tell the GM what you hope they do and roll +WIS. On a 10+, they come in useful numbers. On a 7-9, they come, but hungry, frightened, or demanding.

**Oak-Hearted**
When you endure poison, cold, exhaustion, or harsh travel under open sky, take advantage.

**Fire of the First Men**
When you kindle sacred flame against corruption, rot, or unnatural darkness, it counts as `magical`.

## Advances

- **Dire Form:** When you shapeshift into a predator, your attacks gain `messy`.
- **Stone Circle:** Given time, you can prepare a place where weather magic and spirit-speech come easier.
- **Raven's Eye:** When you send a bird or spirit to scout, ask one extra question after it returns.
- **Storm-Walker:** You and companions moving under natural weather leave less sign and make less noise.
- **Rooted Against Evil:** Demonic fear, illusion, and whispers have disadvantage against you.
- **Improved Stat:** Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +2.
- **Superior Stat** *(Requires: Improved Stat, at least one other advance)*: Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +3.

---

## Drives

- Defend the wild from corruption or exploitation.
- Let the beast, storm, or old magic off its leash.
- Demand respect for an ancient pact or custom.
- Hunt something unnatural across open land.
