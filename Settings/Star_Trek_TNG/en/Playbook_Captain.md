## Background

Pick one.

---

**Diplomatic Prodigy**

You learned early that borders move more easily than people think. When you open formal negotiations with a faction that has heard of Starfleet, say what reputation precedes you and take +1 forward.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you saw me talk our way out of a battle?
- Which of you thinks I care more about peace than justice?
- Which of you knows the envoy I failed to save?
- Which of you still waits for me to break protocol for you?

---

**Frontier Veteran**

You commanded during shortages, isolation, and uncertain maps. When the ship enters an under-supplied or poorly charted region, ask the GM what old instinct warns you first.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you served with me when support never came?
- Which of you has seen me make the hard call and hates me for it?
- Which of you learned endurance from me the hard way?
- Which of you believes I am still fighting an old war?

---

**Political Appointment**

Someone important fought to put you in this chair. When Starfleet Command, an admiralty office, or a planetary government gets involved, say what strings still pull at you and gain leverage with them.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you knows who sponsored my rise?
- Which of you suspects I am here for reasons beyond merit?
- Which of you once covered for my inexperience?
- Which of you expects me to disappoint command on purpose?

---

**Scholar of Civilizations**

Before you commanded a ship, you studied peoples, philosophies, and long arcs of history. When you cite precedent, ritual, or cultural logic to calm a tense scene, take advantage.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you first saw me treat an alien custom with real respect?
- Which of you thinks I romanticize civilizations I barely know?
- Which of you brought me evidence that changed my worldview?
- Which of you asked me a question I still cannot answer?

## Starting Moves

**Make It So**
When you give a clear order in the middle of danger, say who carries it out and roll +CHA. On a 10+, they execute it and choose 1: it is faster than expected, cleaner than expected, or it reveals an opening. On a 7-9, they do it, but the GM chooses a cost, delay, or complication.

**Captain's Log**
When you take a quiet moment to record the truth of the mission, ask the GM one question about the situation, the ship, or your enemy; take +1 forward acting on the answer.

**The Weight of the Chair**
When everyone looks to you for the final call, you may name the principle guiding your decision. If the crew follows it, everyone involved takes +1 forward; if it goes badly, mark XP.

**Formal Reception**
When you meet a leader, dignitary, or representative under ceremonial conditions, roll +INT. On a 10+, ask 3. On a 7-9, ask 1.
- What do they really want from us?
- What etiquette matters here?
- Who in this room resents our presence?
- What could turn this meeting into a crisis?

**Voice of the Federation**
When you invoke Starfleet law, Federation ideals, or your commission to stay a hand or open a door, roll +CHA. On a 10+, they hesitate, comply, or hear you out. On a 7-9, they demand proof, concession, or visible strength first.

**Away Team Override**
When you step onto the away mission yourself, everyone present knows this is no routine survey. Once per mission, you may redirect blame, danger, or attention from another PC onto yourself.

## Advances

- **Steady Under Fire:** When you remain calm in a crisis, allies taking their cue from you have advantage.
- **Trusted by the Admiralty:** Once per session, declare that a command-level contact will answer your message if they plausibly can.
- **Read the Negotiation:** When you **Discern Realities** in a diplomatic scene, ask an extra question.
- **Command Presence:** The first time you speak before hostile witnesses in a scene, take +1 ongoing until violence starts.
- **I Know This Crew:** Once per session, tell another PC what they need to hear; they take advantage if they act on it.
- **Improved Stat:** Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +2.
- **Superior Stat** *(Requires: Improved Stat, at least one other advance)*: Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +3.

## Drives

- Defuse a conflict without firing first.
- Put yourself in danger to uphold Federation principles.
- Trust a member of the crew with something only you could decide.
- Learn something that changes how you see a civilization.
