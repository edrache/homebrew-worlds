## Background

Pick one.

---

**Warp Theory Wunderkind**

You were solving impossible equations before command trusted you with a deck. When you explain a dangerous technical gamble in detail, anyone who follows it knows exactly what failure looks like.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you first realized I was smarter than my rank?
- Which of you still thinks I trust theory too much?
- Which of you watched one of my experiments go catastrophically wrong?
- Which of you backed my work when nobody else would?

---

**Chief of a Broken Ship**

You inherited a mess and made it fly. When you crawl through damaged systems, jury-rigged patches count as proper equipment until the scene changes.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you saw the ship I brought back from near death?
- Which of you knows how little sleep I get in a crisis?
- Which of you learned to trust my ugly repairs?
- Which of you remembers the one system I could not save?

---

**Holodeck Tinkerer**

You have built tools, diagnostics, and test environments out of simulation space. When you prototype a plan in the holodeck, ask the GM what crucial variable your model misses.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you volunteered for my unsafe test?
- Which of you knows my favorite impossible invention?
- Which of you I accidentally embarrassed with a simulation?
- Which of you believes I care more for systems than for people?

---

**Transporter Whisperer**

You know the thin line between clean dematerialization and a nightmare in pattern buffers. When you stabilize a transporter lock under pressure, take advantage.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you trusts me to beam you through hell?
- Which of you was once lost in my pattern buffer?
- Which of you blames me for a transporter accident I could not stop?
- Which of you knows how much I hate using people as test cases?

## Starting Moves

**I Can Keep Her Together**
When the ship suffers damage, overload, or cascading failure and you rush to keep it operational, roll +INT. On a 10+, choose 3. On a 7-9, choose 2.
- It holds longer than anyone expected.
- You isolate the worst of the failure.
- You do not need to sacrifice another vital system.
- You learn what caused it.

**Miracle Worker**
Once per session, declare that you have exactly the component, bypass, or improvised solution needed to attempt a repair that should be impossible.

**Reroute Power**
When you divert power from one system to another, name what weakens and what strengthens. The receiving system works now; the sacrificed system fails, flickers, or creates a new problem until restored.

**Diagnose the Impossible**
When you study a device, anomaly, or system behaving in ways it should not, roll +WIS. On a 10+, ask 3. On a 7-9, ask 1.
- What is this really drawing power from?
- What failure is about to happen?
- What simple assumption is wrong here?
- How could this be made safe enough for now?

**Jefferies Tube Rat**
When you move through maintenance shafts, plasma access, or cramped mechanical spaces, you can always get somewhere important eventually. Roll +DEX if time or danger matters.

**The Engines Know Me**
When you talk to the ship like it can hear you, everyone who hears knows whether you are hopeful, angry, or terrified. If the crew takes that emotion seriously, they take +1 forward.

## Advances

- **Secondary Backup for the Secondary Backup:** Once per session, reveal a hidden workaround already in place.
- **Pattern Expert:** When transporters, replicators, or holodecks go wrong, take advantage.
- **Hands in the Plasma:** Ignore the first point of harm from heat, radiation, or exposed conduits each scene.
- **Chief's Authority:** Engineering crews, technicians, and specialists know your reputation and will hear you out.
- **Not Pretty, but It Works:** Your jury-rigged solutions last a whole mission unless directly broken.
- **Improved Stat:** Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +2.
- **Superior Stat** *(Requires: Improved Stat, at least one other advance)*: Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +3.

## Drives

- Keep the ship running through a crisis nobody planned for.
- Solve a technical mystery before anyone else understands it.
- Protect the crew by taking a dangerous repair on yourself.
- Build something elegant out of panic and scrap.
