# THE ANCIENT MAGE

*You have lived long enough to bury everyone who ever knew you. You weren't distant — you just didn't realize how fast they were going. You are still learning to pay attention.*

**Damage Die:** d6
**HP:** 6 + CON

---

## Name

Pick a name:

**Elf:** Silvane, Tessra, Eulalie, Vorn / Caelith, Merrath, Solven, Astryn
**Ancient Human:** Agatha, Mildred, Cornelius, Vess / Roland, Magnus, Aldric, Bastian
**Northern Wanderer:** Inga, Sigrid, Marta, Hulde / Wendel, Arno, Klaus, Bertold
**Nameless Traveler:** Ash, Pale, Ember, Wander / Dusk, Kestrel, Mire, Veil

---

## Look

Pick one from each row:

**Heritage:** ancient elf | human who found a life-extension spell | wanderer without a home race | ask the table
**Age:** looks young, is ancient | ageless and unsettling | weathered by centuries, not years
**Eyes:** distant and cataloguing | suddenly warm when interested | always measuring how long someone has left
**Build:** slight and unhurried | still and precise | graceful with no wasted motion
**Clothing:** traveler's robes layered over centuries | simple garb repaired a thousand times | scholar's coat with arcane notation stitched in
**Signature:** a grimoire always at hand | the habit of speaking too slowly | a faint smile that arrives late

## Background

Pick one.

---

**Forest Hermit**

You isolated yourself after a loss and spent too many years going nowhere. When you emerge from self-imposed stillness to act on someone else's behalf, take +1 forward.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you found me in isolation and convinced me to leave?
- Which of you knows what I was waiting for in that forest?
- Which of you has seen me grieve in a way I could not explain?
- Which of you reminds me of someone I have already lost?

---

**Pupil of a Legend**

A great mage trained you before they passed. Their methods live in your hands. When you use a technique or philosophy your mentor taught you and describe it aloud, take +1 forward.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you has heard of my mentor by reputation?
- Which of you asked me something my mentor would have asked?
- Which of you noticed me doing something I learned from them?
- Which of you would I most want to introduce to my mentor — if only they were still here?

---

**Mana Suppressor**

You learned long ago to hide your true power. Enemies routinely underestimate you. When you reveal your full mana for the first time in a conflict, take +1 forward and your enemy takes disadvantage on their next action.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you sensed something off about me before I revealed myself?
- Which of you has seen me strike down something much stronger than I appeared?
- Which of you was unsettled when you discovered my true capacity?
- Which of you trusts me more, now that you know what I've been hiding?

---

**Keeper of Statues**

You maintain the markers and monuments of those who came before — heroes, friends, the forgotten. When you return to a place associated with someone you've lost, ask the GM one question about what they did or felt here. The GM answers honestly.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you noticed me pausing at a memorial without explaining why?
- Which of you knows the name of someone whose statue I tend?
- Which of you reminded me, in some small way, of one of the dead?
- Which of you would I want remembered — if something happened to me?

---

## Starting Moves

**World of Visualization**
Magic is only as strong as what you can genuinely imagine. When you cast a spell you have prepared, roll +INT. On a 10+, it works as intended. On a 7-9, it works but choose one: it takes longer than expected, draws unwanted attention, or costs something small.

**Living Grimoire**
You have studied many categories of magic over your long life. Name a type of spell (weather, illusion, fire, binding, transformation, etc.) — you have studied it. You can ask the GM one question about how a spell in that category could help the current situation. The GM answers honestly.

**Ancient Advantage**
When you face something old — a ruin, a demon, a forgotten spell, a buried institution — you have advantage on rolls to understand or interact with it.

**Mana Suppression**
Your true power is hidden. Enemies cannot gauge your strength until you reveal it. The first time you act decisively against something that underestimated you in a scene, take +1 forward.

**Patient Study**
When you spend time quietly observing a creature, person, or magical phenomenon before acting, ask the GM one question about its weakness, motivation, or method. The GM answers honestly.

**Borrowed Time**
The first time each session you watch someone mortal do something selfless or brave, mark XP.

---

## Advances

- **Ancient Grimoire:** Gain access to one unusual spell from a lost magical era. Describe what it does; the GM approves.
- **Mana Flare:** Once per conflict, reveal your full mana — all enemies in sight hesitate, and you take +1 forward.
- **Long Memory:** When something reminds you of the distant past, you may ask one additional question about the current situation.
- **Teach by Doing:** When you model something for an ally rather than explain it, they take +1 forward to their next attempt.
- **The Weight of Years:** Once per session, when you carry grief for someone who died long before anyone else knew them, mark XP.
- **Improved Stat:** Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +2.
- **Superior Stat** *(Requires: Improved Stat, at least one other advance)*: Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +3.

---

## Drives

- Collect a spell you have never seen before.
- Witness a mortal make the most of the time they have.
- Act on a regret you have been carrying for too long.
- Let someone in, even briefly.
