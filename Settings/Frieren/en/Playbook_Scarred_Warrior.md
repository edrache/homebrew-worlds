# THE SCARRED WARRIOR

*You are stronger than you know. That's been the problem. You've spent years telling yourself you're not ready, not enough, not worth it — but the people you care about don't have time for your doubts.*

**Damage Die:** d10
**HP:** 10 + CON

## Background

Pick one.

---

**Survivor's Guilt**

Your home is gone. You lived because someone else didn't. When you fight to protect someone who cannot protect themselves, take +1 forward.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you knows what I ran from?
- Which of you has seen me freeze when I should have acted?
- Which of you reminds me of someone who didn't survive?
- Which of you believes I'm worth something, for reasons I don't yet understand?

---

**Master's Shadow**

A warrior believed in you more than you believed in yourself. They're not here anymore. When you fight in a way that would make your mentor proud, mark XP.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you knew my master, or knows their name?
- Which of you has asked me something my master once asked me?
- Which of you noticed me imitating someone who isn't here?
- Which of you I would most want to bring back stories to?

---

**Dragon Slayer**

You once did something impossible when everyone needed you to. The village knows the story. You don't feel like the person they tell it about. Once per session, when you face something overwhelming and push through anyway, mark XP.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you has heard a version of the story about me?
- Which of you didn't believe it until you saw me fight?
- Which of you knows what I thought about just before I survived?
- Which of you looks at me the same way, whether or not the story is true?

---

**Stagnant Village**

You stayed in one place too long — comfortable, safe, hollow. When you leave behind comfort or safety to keep moving toward something uncertain, mark XP.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you pulled me out of the life I was sleepwalking through?
- Which of you asked me the question I had been avoiding?
- Which of you I worry would be better off without my past following them?
- Which of you reminds me that moving forward is worth the cost?

## Starting Moves

**Overwhelming Strength**
When you use raw physical power — lift, break, throw, or strike — you have advantage.

**Absorb the Blow**
When you take a hit meant for someone nearby, reduce the damage to yourself by 1d6 and they take none.

**Imposter's Fire**
When you fight while doubting yourself out loud — the GM hears it — take +1 forward.

**Fear is a Sharpener**
When you fight something that genuinely terrifies you, deal +1d6 damage on a 10+.

**I Can Keep Up**
When an ally is more experienced or skilled than you, take +1 forward when working directly alongside them.

**Stubborn Body**
When you would be incapacitated, you may keep acting at 1 HP until the end of the scene.

---

## Advances

- **Titan Effort:** Once per session, automatically succeed on a feat of raw physical strength, no roll required.
- **My People Don't Run:** You never take disadvantage from fear-based effects.
- **Crushing Blow:** Your damage die becomes d12 when you attack something that has injured an ally.
- **Remembered Story:** Once per session, tell an ally a story of a warrior from your past. They mark XP.
- **Legacy Warrior:** When you dedicate a kill to someone from your past and name them aloud, take +1 forward for the rest of the scene.
- **Improved Stat:** Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +2.
- **Superior Stat** *(Requires: Improved Stat, at least one other advance)*: Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +3.

---

## Drives

- Kill something your mentor told stories about.
- Protect someone who has given up on protecting themselves.
- Tell a story worth bringing back to someone who matters.
- Admit out loud that you're afraid — and fight anyway.
