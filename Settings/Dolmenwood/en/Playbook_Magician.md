# THE MAGICIAN

*You have read books that were meant to be burned. You intend to read more.*

**Damage Die:** d4
**HP:** 4 + CON

---

## Name

Pick a name:

**Scholarly:** Oswin, Pereth, Valmere, Circe / Aldith, Telvane, Morvain, Cassid
**Self-Taught:** Smoke, Wren, Inkwell, Pale / Crisp, Vane, Dusk, Flint
**Borrowed:** Morrigan, Caelith, Sunderway, Grex / Tavis, Elward, Mirrick, Ash

---

## Look

Pick one from each row:

**Origin:** trained by a master before they disappeared | self-taught from stolen texts | initiated into a secret tradition now dead | found a spell book young and never looked up
**Build:** thin and precise | hunched over notes | still and calculating | always moving, always reaching for something
**Eyes:** cataloguing everything | bright when something rare appears | distant, already thinking three steps ahead | cautious in a way that suggests past catastrophe
**Clothing:** ink-stained robes over practical layers | traveling scholar's coat with arcane notation in the lining | plain garb that hides several concealed pockets | weather-worn academic dress
**Signature:** a spell book always in reach | fingers that move slightly when thinking | a smell of ozone and old parchment | the habit of pausing before speaking to check if someone is listening

## Background

Pick one.

---

**Drune-Adjacent**

You've been close enough to the Hooded Men to learn something. Whether you stole it, traded for it, or ran from them afterward is another matter.

When you use knowledge you acquired from or about the Drune, take +1 forward and tell the GM what it cost you to learn it.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you knows what I took from the Drune?
- Which of you helped me escape after?
- Which of you thinks I should have left well enough alone?
- Which of you doesn't know about this — and I'd like to keep it that way?

---

**Apprentice of a Dead Master**

Your mentor died or disappeared before finishing your education. Their methods live in your hands. Their secrets are still buried somewhere.

When you use a technique or approach your master taught you, describe it aloud and take +1 forward.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you knew my master — or knew of them?
- Which of you was there when I found out they were gone?
- Which of you has seen me do something I could only have learned from someone dangerous?
- Which of you am I looking for something my master left behind?

---

**Collector of Forbidden Books**

You have acquired texts that the Church burns and the Drune hoard. You are very careful about who knows this.

When you consult your collection and ask the GM about something arcane, occult, or historical, the GM answers honestly.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you has seen what I keep in my pack?
- Which of you borrowed something from my collection and hasn't returned it?
- Which of you would report me to the Church if they knew what I had?
- Which of you once asked me to burn a specific book — and I didn't?

---

**Ley Line Reader**

You can feel the Wood's arcane currents — the ley lines, the stones, the places where the world is thinner than it should be. The Drune would kill for your sensitivity. You're hoping they don't find out.

When you enter a new area, you may ask the GM if there is arcane significance here — a ley line, a standing stone, a fairy door, a place of power. The GM answers honestly.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you noticed I went very still when we crossed into this area?
- Which of you has seen me touch a standing stone and pull my hand back quickly?
- Which of you knows I can sense the Drune's workings — and hasn't told anyone?
- Which of you followed me when I went off alone to investigate something?

## Starting Moves

**Cast a Spell**
When you cast a spell you have prepared and visualized, roll +INT. On a 10+, it works as intended. On a 7-9, it works, but choose one: it takes longer than expected | it draws unwanted attention | it costs something small but real.

**Secret Lore**
When you examine something arcane — a spell, an object, a magical effect, a ritual space — ask the GM one question about it. The GM answers honestly.

**Detect Magic**
You can always sense the presence of active magic nearby. When you focus deliberately, the GM tells you the nature of the magic and its source.

**Hoard of Knowledge**
Name a field of arcane study (demonology, fairy runes, ley line theory, crystalmancy, etc.) — you have studied it. Once per subject, ask the GM one question about how your knowledge applies to the current situation. The GM answers honestly.

**Eldritch Items**
You know how to identify and use arcane items. When you attempt to use a magic item of unknown function, roll +INT instead of rolling blind.

**The Price of Power**
When something goes badly wrong with your magic, mark XP.

## Advances

- **New Spell:** Learn one new prepared spell of your description — agree the scope with the GM.
- **Ley Line Tap:** When near a standing stone or ley line nexus, cast one spell without cost.
- **Drune Fragment:** You have acquired a piece of Drune knowledge. It works. It may also be noticed.
- **Analytical Mind:** When you observe a magical effect or creature ability, you may ask the GM its weakness. The GM answers honestly.
- **Arcane Reserves:** Once per session, cast a spell that would normally exhaust you without suffering that cost.
- **Improved Stat:** Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +2.
- **Superior Stat** *(Requires: Improved Stat, at least one other advance)*: Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +3.

---

## Drives

- Acquire a spell or text that no one else has.
- Learn something the Drune have hidden from everyone.
- Cast something you've never tried before, in full knowledge of the risk.
- Find what your master left unfinished — and finish it.
