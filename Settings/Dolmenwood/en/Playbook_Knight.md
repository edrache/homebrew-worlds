# THE KNIGHT

*You serve your liege and your code. When those two things pull in opposite directions, you will face that moment when it comes.*

**Damage Die:** d8
**HP:** 8 + CON

---

Pick a name:

**Noble Born:** Aldric, Margrave, Edric, Isolde / Brennan, Theodric, Vayne, Caelen
**Elevated by Deed:** Strongbow, Ironhand, Blackthorn, Falchion / Goldenshield, Greymantle, Wolfsbane

## Look

Pick one from each row:

**Origin:** born to the nobility | elevated from common blood by deeds | a younger child who needed their own path | sworn in by a dying lord who had no other choice
**Build:** armored, deliberate, and imposing | tall and certain | compact and efficient | scarred in ways that suggest surviving a great deal
**Eyes:** watching for threats to what they protect | weighing honor in every exchange | direct and demanding | quieter than the armor suggests
**Clothing:** plate armor, heavy and well-maintained | campaign-worn armor with a lord's colors | chainmail bearing a noble house's symbol | armor kept immaculate even in the field
**Signature:** a lance or two-handed blade always within reach | the habit of standing between others and a door | a lord's pendant worn openly | the formal courtesy of someone who learned it by necessity

## Background

Pick one.

---

**Sworn to House Harrowmoor**

Lady Harrowmoor's house is steadfast, militant, and facing the encroachment of the Nag-Lord's crookhorns from the north. You serve her with armor and discipline.

When you act directly in defense of House Harrowmoor's lands or people, take +1 forward.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you knows the Harrowmoor lands well enough to know what's at stake?
- Which of you has fought beside me in the northern Wood?
- Which of you thinks the crookhorn threat is worse than my liege believes?
- Which of you has seen me receive orders I disagree with and follow them anyway?

---

**Sworn to House Guillefer**

The Guillefer house is dreamy and aloof, rotating lordship among family members. You serve a house that sometimes forgets you exist — which gives you unusual latitude.

When you act without explicit instructions and it succeeds, mark XP.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you has seen me act without orders — and gotten away with it?
- Which of you knows which Guillefer I'm currently sworn to?
- Which of you do I trust more than my current lord?
- Which of you was there when I last heard from my liege, and knows how long ago that was?

---

**Knight Errant**

You serve no specific house right now. You carry your code without a lord — wandering, doing what a knight does, waiting for a worthy cause or a worthy master.

When you uphold your code in a situation where no one required you to, take +1 forward and mark XP.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you knows why I no longer serve my former liege?
- Which of you has seen me refuse a lord's offer of service?
- Which of you do I actually serve, even if neither of us has named it?
- Which of you has tested my code and watched me hold it?

---

**Disgraced and Returning**

Your knighthood was stripped. You know why. You are earning it back — through deeds or through finding what went wrong and fixing it.

When you act in a way that restores your honor in the eyes of someone who witnessed your disgrace, mark XP.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you knows what I did?
- Which of you has never once used it against me?
- Which of you has seen me be better than the story of my failure?
- Which of you do I most want to witness when I reclaim what I lost?

---

## Starting Moves

**Sworn Vassal**
Name your liege — a noble house, a lord, or a sworn cause. When you act in direct service of your liege's interests, take +1 forward. If you ever betray your liege, lose this move until you perform an act of redemption in their name.

**Armored and Ready**
You are trained in all arms and heavy armor. When you stand your ground in combat while protecting another, you cannot be moved or broken unless overwhelmed by superior numbers.

**Monster Slayer**
When you face a creature of Chaos, an undead, or a supernatural predator, take +1 forward on the first roll you make against it.

**Strength of Will**
When you resist fear, supernatural compulsion, or a breaking of your nerve, roll +STR. On a 10+, you hold firm and take +1 forward. On a 7-9, you hold, but it costs you — mark it.

**Chivalric Code**
You do not use missile weapons. You do not strike the unarmed or surrendering. You name yourself before combat. When you uphold your code and it would have been easier not to, mark XP.

**Mounted**
You have a horse (or can acquire one quickly). When you fight mounted, your damage die increases by one step. Your horse is not expendable.

## Advances

- **Renowned:** Choose a noble house or powerful faction — you are known by name there as someone of worth.
- **Shield of the Innocent:** When you step between an innocent and harm, you take no damage from the first blow.
- **Oath of the House:** Your liege grants you a specific title and territory. Name it — you have responsibilities and resources there.
- **Warlord's Voice:** When you command allies in battle, they take +1 forward on their next action.
- **Unbroken:** Once per session, when you would fall, you don't. You are at 1 HP and conscious.
- **Improved Stat:** Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +2.
- **Superior Stat** *(Requires: Improved Stat, at least one other advance)*: Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +3.

---

## Drives

- Fulfill a duty to your liege at genuine cost to yourself.
- Defeat a creature of Chaos in direct combat.
- Act with honor in a situation that would have let you look away.
- Find out what it means to be a knight when no one is watching.
