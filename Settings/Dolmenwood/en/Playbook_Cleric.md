# THE CLERIC

*You serve God with sword and prayer. The Church needs warriors willing to walk into the dark.*

**Damage Die:** d6
**HP:** 6 + CON

---

Pick one from each row:

**Origin:** raised in the seminary | converted in the field | trained by a wandering order | born to a devout family
**Build:** broad-shouldered and deliberate | lean and wary | scarred from battles fought in God's name | still and composed
**Eyes:** searching for sin | full of quiet certainty | haunted by what they've seen and still believe | steady as a lit candle
**Clothing:** church-issued plate, worn but maintained | campaign-battered armor with holy symbols etched in | heavy robes over chain | clean and precise regardless of the mud
**Signature:** a Chapes pendant of St Clewyd always at hand | knuckles rough from both prayer and fighting | a scar across the throat from something that almost worked | a field journal of sins witnessed and forgiven

## Background

Pick one.

---

**Servant of the Order of St Faxis**

You answer directly to Bishop Sanguine's inquisitorial arm — rooting out occultism wherever you find it. You know the faces of the Hooded Men, or you're learning them.

When you act openly against agents of the Drune, the Cold Prince, or the Nag-Lord, take +1 forward.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you has seen me hesitate before something I was ordered to destroy?
- Which of you knows something about my mission that I haven't told my superiors?
- Which of you do I trust more than the Order — and why?
- Which of you was I sent to watch?

---

**Keeper of a Lost Shrine**

A shrine in the deep Wood was in your care. Something took it from you — flood, fire, Drune, or something worse. You haven't stopped looking.

When you recover a relic, shrine, or sacred object for the Church, mark XP.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you has helped me search for what I lost?
- Which of you knows what happened at the shrine — even if you haven't said so?
- Which of you lost something they haven't recovered either?
- Which of you doubts I'll ever find it?

---

**Fallen from Grace**

You transgressed. You've done your penance, and you walk in Lawful service again — but you remember what the fall felt like.

When you confront another who stands at the edge of disgrace, take +1 forward to help or hinder them.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you knows what I did?
- Which of you has never judged me for it?
- Which of you is close to the same edge I once stood on?
- Which of you caught me doing something I promised I'd never do again?

---

**Field Consecrated**

You were never trained in a seminary. You were consecrated by a dying priest in a bog and told to carry on. You've been carrying on ever since.

When you consecrate a space — a makeshift altar, a dying man's chest, a fallen shrine — the GM gives you one true piece of information about what has happened here.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you was there when I was consecrated?
- Which of you thinks I'm not a real Cleric?
- Which of you has seen me perform a rite that shouldn't have worked — but did?
- Which of you have I buried?

---

## Starting Moves

**Holy Vow**
You are Lawful or Neutral. If you act against your vow or become Chaotic, you lose the ability to use *Pray for Grace* until you perform an act of atonement. The GM tells you what that requires.

**Pray for Grace**
When you pray in the name of a saint before taking action, roll +WIS. On a 10+, the GM gives you an honest answer to one question about the situation, and take +1 forward. On a 7-9, take +1 forward, but the GM asks something of you in return.

**Turn the Unholy**
When you confront an undead creature, demon, or creature of Chaos with holy authority, roll +WIS. On a 10+, it flees, is destroyed, or cannot approach you. On a 7-9, it hesitates — you and your allies can act before it does.

**Holy Warrior**
You are trained in arms and armor. When you fight in defense of the innocent or in direct service of the Church, take +1 forward.

**Saint's Memory**
You know the deeds, relics, and shrines of the saints of the Pluritine Church. When you identify a saint's relic or shrine, ask the GM one question about its history, power, or location. The GM answers honestly.

**Carry the Light**
When an ally would break under fear, grief, or despair, you may give them your certainty instead. They take +1 forward. You mark XP.

## Advances

- **Holy Order:** Choose a patron saint. Once per session, invoke their specific power — described between you and the GM.
- **Blessed Arms:** Your weapon carries the saint's mark. It counts as holy against undead and creatures of Chaos.
- **Exorcism:** When you perform a rite of exorcism on a person or place, roll +WIS. On a 10+, the corruption is expelled. On a 7-9, it is weakened — but it remembers you now.
- **Unwavering:** You are immune to magical fear and compulsion that originates from Chaos or Fairy.
- **Word of Judgement:** Once per conflict, speak a holy condemnation. All enemies who hear it take disadvantage on their next action.
- **Improved Stat:** Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +2.
- **Superior Stat** *(Requires: Improved Stat, at least one other advance)*: Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +3.

---

## Drives

- Recover a lost shrine and return it to the Church.
- Stand between an innocent and something that would corrupt them.
- Confront a member of the Drune or the Nag-Lord's court and prevail.
- Act from genuine faith, not duty, at a moment when it costs you something.
