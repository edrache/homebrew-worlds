# THE FRIAR

*You carry no weapon. Only faith, two good hands, and a knowledge of what grows in the deep Wood.*

**Damage Die:** d4
**HP:** 4 + CON

---

Pick a name:

**Church Names:** Brother Ancel, Sister Maren, Brother Godwin, Sister Petry
**Given Names:** Wulfric, Elsa, Torin, Sanna / Addo, Mira, Cob, Fenn
**Road Names:** Grey, Mudfoot, Tallow, Saltmarsh / Slow Hand, Thornberry, Lightpurse

## Look

Pick one from each row:

**Origin:** seminary trained but went wandering | self-ordained after a divine experience | travelling since before taking formal vows | converted late, still learning the prayers
**Build:** wiry and weathered from the road | soft-spoken and slight | broad and calm, moves slowly on purpose | slight with quick hands for tending wounds
**Eyes:** open and inviting confession | sad in a way they don't talk about | looking for signs of God in everything | steady in a crisis
**Clothing:** plain dark robes, worn and often muddy | rough spun habit with a saint's pendant | layered travelling clothes with a wooden prayer token | simple wool, always smelling faintly of herbs
**Signature:** a large pack of herbs and remedies | the habit of asking how people are and meaning it | a small carved saint's figure worn smooth from handling | always knowing where the nearest source of water is

## Background

Pick one.

---

**Wandering Preacher**

You travel the Wood spreading the word of God to those in isolated hamlets and lonely crossroads. You've been welcomed and turned away, sometimes at the same door.

When you tend to people of little means — farmers, woodcutters, the isolated — and ask nothing in return, mark XP.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you has seen me refused at a village gate?
- Which of you followed me away from a settlement that needed something I couldn't give?
- Which of you has listened to me preach, even once?
- Which of you thinks the Church I serve is not what I believe it to be?

---

**Herbalist of the Deep Wood**

You learned what grows in Dolmenwood — the healing fungi, the strange mosses, the herbs that cure and the ones that kill if used wrong. Some of your knowledge came from sources the Church would not approve.

When you forage in the Wood, roll +WIS. On a 10+, you find what you're looking for and something useful you weren't. On a 7-9, you find what you need, but it takes time or takes you somewhere you'd rather not have gone.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you has benefited from my knowledge of what grows here?
- Which of you has seen the stranger parts of my collection?
- Which of you knows I sometimes learn from the witches, even if I'd never say so?
- Which of you has watched me hesitate before using something I knew would work?

---

**Shrine Pilgrim**

You walk from shrine to shrine throughout the Wood — some lost, some found, some things others would not call shrines at all. You collect prayers like other people collect coins.

When you pray at a shrine — even a ruined or informal one — the GM gives you one true piece of information about the surrounding area.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you has walked beside me on a pilgrimage leg?
- Which of you found a shrine and told me about it before telling anyone else?
- Which of you thinks my pilgrimage is an excuse to go somewhere?
- Which of you has sat quietly while I prayed, without asking why?

---

**Penitent**

You did something. The Church knows what it was. You are walking your penance out here in the Wood, doing good wherever you can, in hope that one day the debt is paid.

When you perform an act of genuine selfless aid at personal cost, mark XP.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you knows what I did?
- Which of you has never once brought it up?
- Which of you do I suspect is also doing penance for something?
- Which of you have I helped enough that I think it's starting to matter?

## Starting Moves

**Healer's Hands**
When you tend to an injured person with herbs, prayer, or care, roll +WIS. On a 10+, they recover as much as they safely can and take +1 forward. On a 7-9, they recover, but the healing costs you something — supplies, time, or a price the GM names.

**Herbal Knowledge**
You know what grows in Dolmenwood. When you forage for herbs or fungi, you always find something useful. When you identify a plant or fungus, ask the GM one question about its properties. The GM answers honestly.

**Minor Holy Magic**
Once per day, you may call on a saint's blessing. Describe the need and the saint. Roll +WIS. On a 10+, the effect happens cleanly. On a 7-9, it happens, but with a complication the GM names.

**Turn the Unholy**
When you confront an undead creature or spirit with prayer and conviction, roll +WIS. On a 10+, it cannot approach you and retreats. On a 7-9, it hesitates long enough for you to act.

**Gospel Road**
When you speak honestly with someone in need — not preaching, just speaking — roll +CHA. On a 10+, they open up, and you may ask the GM one question about what they know or carry. On a 7-9, they soften, but there's still something they won't tell you.

**Trusted Presence**
People tell you things. Common folk, frightened farmers, even folk who distrust the Church — they find you easier to speak to than most. When you listen without judgment, mark XP.

## Advances

- **Saint's Blessing:** Choose a patron saint. Learn one powerful prayer specific to their domain — describe it with the GM.
- **Forest Medicine:** When you treat wounds in the field using found materials, the result is as effective as any trained surgeon.
- **Consecrate:** You can consecrate a makeshift shrine in any location. While within its bounds, allies cannot be targeted by the undead.
- **The Long Road:** You are never lost in the Wood. You always know the way to the nearest settlement.
- **Unarmed and Unbothered:** You can never be the first one attacked in an encounter — enemies see no threat in you, until you act.
- **Improved Stat:** Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +2.
- **Superior Stat** *(Requires: Improved Stat, at least one other advance)*: Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +3.

---

## Drives

- Heal someone who didn't believe they could be helped.
- Find a lost shrine and restore it to use.
- Give something you need to someone who needs it more.
- Act from compassion rather than duty, at a moment when the difference matters.
