# THE HUNTER

*You know this Wood. You know what moves through it. You know what has gone in and not come back.*

**Damage Die:** d8
**HP:** 8 + CON

---

## Name

Pick a name:

**Common Names:** Aldric, Wulfric, Maren, Sanna / Bram, Cob, Elsie, Holt
**Forest Names:** Bracken, Ash, Thorn, Rowan / Grey, Fen, Birch, Silvan
**Earned Names:** Longsight, Quietfoot, Ironbow, Trackless / Mudwalker, Stillhand, Coldsnap, Duskwarden

---

## Look

Pick one from each row:

**Origin:** raised on the wood's edge by a hunter family | trained as a bounty hunter for the noble houses | learned alone, through loss | former soldier who chose the trees over the road
**Build:** lean and patient | wiry and low to the ground | still until something moves, then very fast | deliberate in everything
**Eyes:** always checking the distance | reading terrain, not faces | scanning for what shouldn't be there | calm in a way that comes from having been afraid many times
**Clothing:** practical wood-wear, earth-toned and worn quiet | forest-weathered layers with nothing that catches | hunting leathers marked by what you've tracked | a mix of function and personal history — trophies, tokens, repairs
**Signature:** an animal companion always nearby | the habit of crouching to read sign where others just walk past | a longbow or crossbow maintained to exactness | a quiver with notches cut for each significant hunt

## Background

Pick one.

---

**Tracker of Crookhorns**

You know the Nag-Lord's corrupted breggles better than most — their tracks, their patterns, their corrupted rage. You've been hunting them in the northern reaches before anyone else thought to worry.

When you track or hunt crookhorns or creatures bearing the Nag-Lord's corruption, take +1 forward.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you has been in the northern Wood with me?
- Which of you doesn't understand yet how bad it's getting up there?
- Which of you has lost someone to what I've been hunting?
- Which of you trusts my read on what's moving through this area?

---

**Bounty Hunter of the Noble Houses**

You work contracts — monsters, criminals, missing persons, things lords want found or stopped. You know the great houses, their territories, and what they pay for.

When you take a contract from a noble house and pursue it, take +1 forward to all moves made in direct service of that contract.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you came with me on a previous contract?
- Which of you knows which house has a standing offer on something I'm not ready to collect?
- Which of you is the reason I turned down a contract once?
- Which of you has been a target — and doesn't know I was the one asked?

---

**Animal Bonded**

Your companion found you, not the other way around. It was injured, or lost, or old enough to choose. You've been together long enough that the bond is deep and wordless.

When your companion acts on your behalf without instruction, the GM tells you what it has sensed that you haven't.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you has seen my companion do something that made you reconsider what animals understand?
- Which of you knows my companion's name?
- Which of you has my companion warmed to, without my prompting?
- Which of you has my companion made clear they distrust — and have they been right?

---

**Forest Recluse Returning**

You lived alone in the deep Wood for a long time. You know things about it that people who live near it don't. You've come back out for a reason, and the reason matters to you.

When you draw on knowledge gained from solitary time in the deep Wood, ask the GM one question about the terrain, creatures, or hidden things in the area. The GM answers honestly.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you found me in the Wood, or was there when I came back out?
- Which of you knows what sent me into the forest in the first place?
- Which of you has seen me look at something out there like it's not quite right — and say nothing?
- Which of you do I stay for?

---

## Starting Moves

**Animal Companion**
You have a loyal animal companion — a large dog, a hawk, a wildcat, a trained crow, or something else agreed with the GM. Name it and describe it. When you act in concert with your companion, take +1 forward. If your companion is injured, you may not take +1 from any move until they are healed. If your companion dies, mark a Debility and do not name a new one until you've grieved.

**Track**
When you follow a trail — creature, person, or event — roll +WIS. On a 10+, you follow it cleanly and learn something about what made it. On a 7-9, you follow it, but the trail is harder to read or the quarry knows it's being followed.

**Alertness**
You are never surprised. When the GM would call for a check against ambush or sudden threat, you may act first.

**Marksman**
When you make a ranged attack from a position of advantage or after taking a moment to aim, take +1 forward.

**Wayfinding**
You cannot be lost in the Wood. When the party is in danger of losing their way, you find the path. When entering a new area, ask the GM one of: What is the most significant danger here? What passes through this area regularly? Where would someone go to not be found?

**Patient Hunter**
When you spend time quietly observing a creature, a location, or a person before acting, ask the GM one question: what is their weakness, routine, or vulnerability? The GM answers honestly.

## Advances

- **Bonded Senses:** Your companion can relay what it has sensed. Once per session, ask the GM one question about what your companion has observed in your absence.
- **Lethal Shot:** When you make a ranged attack from concealment against a target that doesn't know you're there, your damage ignores armor.
- **Wilderness Medicine:** When you treat wounds using the Wood's natural resources, the effect is equivalent to professional medical treatment.
- **Monster Lore:** Choose a type of creature common to Dolmenwood — crookhorns, undead, fairy creatures, fungi-things. You have expertise on this type. When you act against them, take +1 forward.
- **Vanishing Act:** You can disappear into the Wood at will. When pursued, roll +WIS. On a 10+, pursuers lose you completely. On a 7-9, you lose them temporarily — they may pick up the trail again.
- **Improved Stat:** Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +2.
- **Superior Stat** *(Requires: Improved Stat, at least one other advance)*: Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +3.

---

## Drives

- Hunt something no one else has been able to track down.
- Protect the deep Wood from something that doesn't belong in it.
- Understand what your companion knows that you don't.
- Go somewhere in the Wood that should be avoided — and come back.
