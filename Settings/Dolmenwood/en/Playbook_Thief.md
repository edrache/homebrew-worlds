# THE THIEF

*You know where the doors are. You know which ones were never meant to be found.*

**Damage Die:** d6
**HP:** 4 + CON

---

## Name

Pick a name:

**Used Names:** Pip, Wren, Tallow, Crib / Ash, Hook, Slate, Marten
**Birth Names:** Edmund, Nessa, Corvin, Sable / Aldith, Torin, Wex, Prue
**Professional Names:** The Pale Hand, Minnow, Quickfingers, Sundew / Greyrat, Sparrow, Locksworth, Fitch

## Look

Pick one from each row:

**Origin:** born in the alleys of Dreg | trained by a guild that may or may not still exist | self-taught from necessity | came to this from something more respectable
**Build:** slight and unobtrusive | quick and low-center | average in every way that helps | wiry and flexible in ways that matter
**Eyes:** cataloguing exits | present everywhere, registered nowhere | warm on the surface, counting the silver | watching hands, not faces
**Clothing:** nondescript travelling clothes with hidden pockets | dark layers, comfortable | well-dressed compared to the situation | worn and forgettable on purpose
**Signature:** thieves' tools always accessible | the habit of sitting where they can see the door | never touching anything without knowing what it is first | always knowing three ways out

## Background

Pick one.

---

**Tomb Robber of the Deep Wood**

You make your living from what the dead left behind — in the barrow mounds, the crumbling fairy ruins, the abandoned delvings no one else is willing to enter. You've learned a great deal about what's down there.

When you enter a ruin, tomb, or abandoned structure, ask the GM one of: What is the real danger here? What was this place originally? Where is the most valuable thing likely to be?

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you has been inside a ruin with me before?
- Which of you once talked me out of opening something I shouldn't have?
- Which of you knows how I got this specific scar?
- Which of you have I given a share to when I didn't have to?

---

**Dreg Operative**

You came up in Dreg — the port town, the river trade, Madame Shantywood's shadow economy. You know how the underside of things works and who runs what.

When you deal with criminal elements, river traders, smugglers, or the unofficial economy of Dolmenwood, take +1 forward.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you knows the name Madame Shantywood?
- Which of you got me out of Dreg the last time things went wrong there?
- Which of you has done work for the same people I have?
- Which of you doesn't know what I am — and I'd like to keep it that way?

---

**Con Artist and Charlatan**

You've sold false relics to devout believers, fake maps to desperate explorers, and invented entire identities for single evenings. It's behind you. Mostly.

When you maintain a false identity or deception for longer than an hour without slipping, mark XP.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you have I deceived in the past?
- Which of you knows my real name?
- Which of you caught me in a lie and said nothing?
- Which of you do I make no effort to deceive?

---

**Guild Dropout**

You were trained by a guild — how to move, how to case, how to disappear. Then something happened and you're on your own. The guild may or may not be looking for you.

When you use techniques clearly learned from formal training, take +1 forward and tell the GM what it reveals about your past.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you knows I was trained by a guild?
- Which of you doesn't know — and I've been actively hiding it?
- Which of you was there when I separated from them?
- Which of you knows whether they're still looking for me?

## Starting Moves

**Backstab**
When you strike from hiding or when your target doesn't know they're in danger, roll +DEX. On a 10+, you deal your damage and choose one: you remain hidden | you create an opening for an ally | they cannot raise an alarm. On a 7-9, you deal your damage, but you're exposed.

**Slip the Lock**
When you pick a lock, disarm a trap, or bypass a physical barrier, roll +DEX. On a 10+, you handle it cleanly and quickly. On a 7-9, you get it done, but choose one: it takes longer than expected | something is triggered that wasn't visible | you leave evidence behind.

**Shadow Movement**
When you move unseen through an area, roll +DEX. On a 10+, you pass without trace. On a 7-9, you get through, but someone almost noticed, or you had to leave something behind to do it.

**Decipher Script**
You can read codes, ciphers, thieves' marks, and old documents. When you examine a written text, you can usually extract the meaning — and what it's hiding.

**Silver Tongue**
When you lie, misdirect, or fast-talk someone who has no reason to distrust you, roll +CHA. On a 10+, they believe you or do what you suggest. On a 7-9, they mostly believe you, but ask one question you must answer.

**Always Watching**
When you enter a new space, you always know the exits, the sightlines, and — with a moment of quiet attention — the most likely hiding places for both people and things.

## Advances

- **Poison Knowledge:** You know the common toxins available in Dolmenwood, how to acquire them, and how to apply them without being noticed.
- **Second Identity:** You have a complete false identity — a name, a story, and a contact who can confirm it. It holds until you do something that breaks it.
- **Fence:** You know a buyer for almost anything in Dolmenwood. When you have something to sell, you can find a buyer and get a fair price.
- **Vanish:** Once per scene, you may disappear from a situation — not magically, but thoroughly. You are simply gone before anyone can stop you.
- **Read the Hidden:** When you observe a person carefully, ask the GM what they're hiding and what they most want. The GM answers honestly.
- **Improved Stat:** Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +2.
- **Superior Stat** *(Requires: Improved Stat, at least one other advance)*: Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +3.

## Drives

- Take something from someone who doesn't deserve to have it.
- Find what's buried in a ruin everyone else passed by.
- Get out of a situation that should have been inescapable.
- Be trusted by someone who has good reason not to trust you.
