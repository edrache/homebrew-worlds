# THE BARD

*You know the stories that keep people alive in the dark. You know when a new one is starting.*

**Damage Die:** d6
**HP:** 6 + CON

---

## Name

Pick a name:

**Court Names:** Silvaine, Aldric, Meredith, Corslyn / Evrett, Beatrix, Tamsin, Rowan
**Road Names:** Pip, Wren, Gorse, Holly / Reed, Sparrow, Latch, Flint
**Stage Names:** Brightbow, Silvertongue, The Crow, Nightwatch / Lowbell, Ashwood, Trueblood, Rambler

---

## Look

Pick one from each row:

**Origin:** trained at a noble court | self-taught on the road | apprentice of a wandering singer | inherited the craft from a family tradition
**Build:** light and quick | still and watchful, performing only when needed | expressive and vivid | unhurried, conserving everything for the right moment
**Eyes:** always reading the room | lit up when a good story starts | recording everything for later | kind to people, harder on ideas
**Clothing:** travelling performer's outfit, worn and charming | rich fabric gone slightly shabby | practical layers with a colorful detail | simple garb that somehow draws the eye
**Signature:** a stringed instrument, always in reach | the habit of humming while thinking | a notebook crammed with overheard dialogue and half-remembered songs | a long scarf or cloak used for emphasis

---

## Background

Pick one.

---

**Court Performer**

You know the great houses — their colors, their grudges, their vanities. You've performed for at least one of them. You know what they want to hear and what they pretend not to hear.

When you navigate noble politics, court etiquette, or a gathering of the highborn, take +1 forward.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you has seen me perform at a noble's table?
- Which of you knows which house I'm no longer welcome in?
- Which of you warned me before I said something dangerous?
- Which of you doesn't know about my history with the nobility?

---

**Folklore Collector**

You travel Dolmenwood gathering old stories — the ones told around winter fires, the ones people stop telling when strangers are present. You have heard versions of stories that people thought were lost.

When you draw on folklore to understand a creature, place, or event, ask the GM one question. The GM answers honestly.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you told me a story I'd never heard before?
- Which of you thinks what I collect is more dangerous than I believe?
- Which of you has seen me recognize something in the real world from a story I knew?
- Which of you gave me the strangest piece of folklore you've heard?

---

**Fairy Tale Survivor**

Something happened to you in the Wood that most people only hear about in stories. You walked out of it. You don't always know why.

When you recognize that the current situation follows a fairy tale pattern, tell the GM what you think the pattern is. If you're right, take +1 forward and mark XP.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you knows what happened to me in the Wood?
- Which of you has seen me go very still when something feels familiar?
- Which of you thinks I'm not as recovered from it as I appear?
- Which of you would I tell the whole story to, if I ever did?

---

**Cautionary Tale**

Your most famous performance caused a problem. You were there. You've been more careful since — or you're trying to be.

When you hold back rather than act impulsively, take +1 forward to the next thing you do instead.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you knows the story of what I did?
- Which of you has never asked, and I'm grateful for it?
- Which of you caught me almost doing the same thing again?
- Which of you am I most afraid to disappoint?

---

## Starting Moves

**Counter Charm**
When you resist fairy magic, enchantment, or supernatural compulsion, roll +CHA. On a 10+, you are unaffected and may act freely. On a 7-9, you resist for now — but the effect lingers, and the GM tells you what it will cost to stay free.

**Enchanting Performance**
When you perform — music, story, or poetry — for an audience, roll +CHA. On a 10+, choose two: they are moved, they lower their guard, they remember something, they tell you something they wouldn't otherwise. On a 7-9, choose one.

**Folklore**
You have extensive knowledge of the legends, creatures, and history of Dolmenwood. When you draw on that knowledge about a creature, place, faction, or event, ask the GM one honest question about it.

**Decipher Old Script**
You can read archaic forms of Woldish, old liturgical texts, and runic inscriptions found in ruins and on standing stones. When you examine an inscription, the GM tells you what it says and may tell you more.

**Read the Room**
When you carefully observe a social situation before acting, ask the GM one of: who holds the real power here? What does someone want that they haven't said? Where is the tension that could break this apart?

**The Story Continues**
The first time each session that something goes badly and a companion is about to give up, you may offer a story that puts it in a different frame. They take +1 forward, and you mark XP.

---

## Advances

- **Ballad of the Wood:** Compose a song about a real event from the campaign. While that song is known, allies who hear it take +1 forward to acts that mirror its theme.
- **Fairy Tongue:** You have learned enough of the fairy language to speak basic pleasantries and identify glamours when you hear them.
- **Known by Name:** Choose a noble house or powerful faction in Dolmenwood — you are known there, positively. Use this when it matters.
- **Disarming Charm:** When you make a positive first impression on someone, they cannot bring themselves to harm you until you give them a reason.
- **Living Archive:** Once per session, recall a piece of folklore that directly applies to the current problem. The GM tells you one true thing you've remembered at the right moment.
- **Improved Stat:** Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +2.
- **Superior Stat** *(Requires: Improved Stat, at least one other advance)*: Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +3.

---

## Drives

- Learn a story that was thought lost forever.
- Perform in front of someone who has the power to change what happens next.
- Recognize a fairy tale pattern while it's happening — and change the ending.
- Tell a hard truth through a story when saying it directly would have failed.
