## Background

Pick one.

---

**Academy Blade of Throal**

You trained in the dwarven kingdom's finest sword-school, where technique is catalogued like engineering and every stance has a name in three languages. When you study an opponent's fighting style before engaging, ask the GM one: what training have they had, what habit will betray them, or what are they afraid of?

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you has watched me practice and understood what you saw?
- Which of you thinks discipline is a cage rather than a gift?
- Which of you did I once correct on how to hold a weapon?
- Which of you do I consider a worthy sparring partner?

---

**Wandering Blade-Singer**

You travel from town to ruined town, trading performances and duels for lodging and legend. When you perform a display of swordsmanship before an audience, roll +CHA. On a 10+, choose 2. On a 7-9, choose 1.
- They offer shelter, food, or information freely.
- A potential patron, rival, or lover reveals themselves.
- Word of your skill spreads; take +1 forward with people who heard of the performance.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you saw one of my performances and was moved by it?
- Which of you thinks my art is frivolous in a world still scarred by Horrors?
- Which of you do I want to compose a blade-song about?
- Which of you carries a wound I gave in a duel I regret?

---

**Duellist of a Fallen Court**

You served a noble house that was destroyed during or after the Scourge. Your blade remembers the forms of a court that no longer exists. When you invoke the etiquette of the old courts to demand a formal duel, challenge, or contest of honor, your opponent must accept or lose face before all present.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you knows the name of the house I served?
- Which of you has tried to convince me to let the past die?
- Which of you do I secretly wish had been born in time to see the old court?
- Which of you did I challenge before I understood them?

---

**Thread-Marked Seeker**

You pursue the art of the perfect strike -- a single blow woven with thread magic into something transcendent. You have woven a thread to your blade. Name it. Once per session, when you land a decisive blow, describe a brief flash of the thread's history or the weapon's legend. Mark XP.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you has felt the hum of my blade's thread and been unsettled?
- Which of you believes thread magic is dangerous and arrogant?
- Which of you do I trust to carry my blade if I fall?
- Which of you witnessed the moment I first wove the thread?

## Starting Moves

**Riposte**
When you parry an enemy's attack and immediately counter-strike, roll +DEX. On a 10+, you avoid their blow entirely and deal your damage. On a 7-9, you trade blows -- deal your damage but take theirs as well.

**Maneuver**
When you use superior footwork, feints, or positioning to gain tactical advantage in melee, roll +DEX. On a 10+, choose 2. On a 7-9, choose 1.
- You force your opponent where you want them -- a ledge, a corner, a companion's reach.
- You create an opening; take +1 forward on your next attack.
- You keep your guard flawless; take +1 armor until you move or attack.

**Taunt**
When you insult, mock, or challenge a foe's skill to draw their attention and fury onto yourself, roll +CHA. On a 10+, they focus on you completely; allies acting against them take advantage. On a 7-9, you have their attention, but they come at you harder than expected -- the GM will say how.

**Blade Meditation**
When you spend a quiet moment with your blade -- cleaning it, balancing it, speaking to it -- choose one: heal 1d4 HP, clear a debility related to fear or despair, or ask the GM what your instincts tell you about the nearest danger.

**Assess the Duel**
When you cross blades with a new opponent for the first time, you may ask the GM one: are they better than me, what is their signature move, or what will they sacrifice to win?

**Flourish**
When you perform a dazzling display of bladework in the middle of combat -- spinning, cutting the air, or executing a perfect form -- choose one ally. They take +1 forward, inspired. If Horrors, spirits, or magical beings are watching, they must acknowledge your artistry; the GM will tell you how they react.

## Advances

- **First Blood:** When you deal damage to a foe who has not yet been wounded in this fight, deal +1d4 damage.
- **Thread Sight:** You can sense the presence of active threads -- magical bonds between Namegivers and items, places, or patterns. You cannot read them, only feel them.
- **Perfect Parry:** Once per fight, you may completely negate one melee attack against you. Describe the technique.
- **Sword's Philosophy:** When you counsel someone about fear, loss, or purpose using a lesson from bladework, roll +WIS. On a 10+, they take +1 forward and you mark XP. On a 7-9, the lesson stings -- they take +1 forward but resent you for it.
- **Dance of Death:** When you fight two or more foes alone, take +1 armor as long as you keep moving and do not stop to help anyone else.
- **Improved Stat:** Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +2.
- **Superior Stat** *(Requires: Improved Stat, at least one other advance)*: Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +3.

## Drives

- Seek out a worthy opponent and face them blade-to-blade.
- Protect someone who cannot defend themselves using only your skill.
- Learn the name, history, or legend of a weapon, technique, or fallen master.
- Demonstrate that art and beauty still matter in a world scarred by Horrors.
