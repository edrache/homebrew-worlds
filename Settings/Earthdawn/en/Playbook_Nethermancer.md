## Background

Pick one.

---

**Kaer Nethermancer**

You were your kaer's watcher against the Horrors, the one who tested the wards from inside. When you examine a sealed place, ward, or magical barrier, ask the GM one: what breached it, what holds it together, or what waits on the other side.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you lived in the kaer I protected?
- Which of you has seen the marks the Scourge left on me?
- Which of you trusts my judgment about Horror corruption?
- Which of you believes my vigil broke something inside me?

---

**Astral Cartographer**

You map the hidden terrain of astral space, charting corruption, spirit currents, and thread anchors. When you spend time studying the astral landscape of a location, take +1 forward to any move involving magic, spirits, or Horror-taint in that area.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you has carried one of my astral charts and seen it prove true?
- Which of you wandered into a place I warned was corrupted?
- Which of you thinks I see things that are not there?
- Which of you wants me to read the astral marks on something they carry?

---

**Spirit Broker**

You negotiate with the dead as others negotiate at market. When you offer a spirit something it wants -- a name spoken, a debt settled, a body buried -- it must answer one question honestly or perform one small service before departing.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you has a dead companion whose spirit I have spoken with?
- Which of you owes a debt to the dead that I helped broker?
- Which of you thinks dealing with spirits is a path to Horror-mark?
- Which of you watched me pay a price they thought was too high?

---

**Horror Stalker**

You have touched Horror-taint and walked away changed. When you sense a Horror or its construct for the first time, the GM tells you one thing about its nature, its weakness, or its hunger.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you was there when I first touched the Horror-taint?
- Which of you checks me for corruption when they think I am not looking?
- Which of you knows that what I hunt also hunts me?
- Which of you would put me down if I turned?

## Starting Moves

**Astral Sight**
When you open your perception to the astral plane, roll +INT. On a 10+, the GM describes the astral condition of your surroundings: threads, corruption, spirit presence, magical wards. On a 7-9, you see clearly, but something in astral space notices you looking.

**Summon Ally Spirit**
When you call a spirit from the netherworld and bind it to service, roll +INT. On a 10+, it appears and serves faithfully for the scene -- choose its nature: messenger, guardian, or spy. On a 7-9, it serves, but it demands something: a memory, a drop of blood, or a future favor.

**Spirit Tongue**
When you speak with the dead -- a corpse, a haunt, or a lingering presence -- you may ask them two questions. They must answer, though the dead remember imperfectly and see through the lens of their last emotions.

**Horror Ward**
When you inscribe protective sigils against Horror influence on a person, object, or threshold, roll +INT. On a 10+, the ward holds strong -- the next Horror-taint, possession attempt, or astral intrusion targeting the warded subject fails. On a 7-9, the ward holds but cracks: it will stop one attempt, then break.

**Thread Weaving**
When you weave a magical thread between yourself and a place, object, or willing person, mark the connection. While the thread holds, you can sense strong emotions, danger, or corruption affecting the other end, regardless of distance. You can maintain threads equal to your INT (minimum 1).

**Nethermantic Bolt**
When you channel raw netherworld energy at a target, deal your damage at `near`. The bolt is cold, dark, and visible in astral space.

## Advances

- **Banish the Unbound:** When you confront a free-roaming spirit or astral entity and command it to depart, roll +INT. On a 10+, it is driven back across the veil. On a 7-9, it goes, but it remembers you.
- **Blood Oath Reading:** When you taste blood willingly given, you learn one truth about the giver: their deepest fear, their strongest bond, or whether they bear Horror-taint.
- **Astral Fortress:** When you fortify a camp or shelter with astral wards, the group cannot be surprised by spirits, undead, or Horror constructs while resting there.
- **Deeper Sight:** When you use **Astral Sight**, on a 12+ you may also ask the GM: what is hidden here, what was here before, or what thread connects this place to another?
- **Speak with Horror:** You may attempt to communicate with a Horror or its construct. The GM will tell you what it wants. This does not make it friendly.
- **Improved Stat:** Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +2.
- **Superior Stat** *(Requires: Improved Stat, at least one other advance)*: Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +3.

## Drives

- Uncover the truth about a Horror, spirit, or astral mystery.
- Protect someone from corruption they do not yet see.
- Use the fear others have of your discipline to achieve something without violence.
- Pay a personal cost to keep the netherworld from spilling into the living world.
