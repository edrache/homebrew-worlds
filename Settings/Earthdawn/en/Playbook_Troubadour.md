# TROUBADOUR

*You carry the names the Scourge tried to devour, and when you speak them aloud the world remembers what it was.*

**Damage Die:** d6
**HP:** 6 + CON

## Background

Pick one.

---

**Kaer Chronicler**

You were born underground, memorizing the tales of Before so they would survive. When you recite established lore to fill a gap in knowledge, the GM will tell you something extra that the original tellers hid between the lines.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you emerged from the same kaer as me, or one nearby?
- Which of you carries a story I have not yet recorded?
- Which of you doubts the histories I preserve?
- Which of you has seen me weep over a tale that ended badly?

---

**Thread Weaver**

You sense the invisible threads that bind Names to the world. When you study an item, place, or person of significance, ask the GM: what thread was broken here, or what thread is waiting to be woven?

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you has a thread I can feel but cannot yet read?
- Which of you has asked me to trace the pattern of something lost?
- Which of you fears what I might find woven into their Name?
- Which of you has felt the pull when I weave a thread nearby?

---

**Horror Namer**

You learned that naming a Horror weakens it, and silence feeds it. The first time each session you speak the true Name of a Horror, a corrupt influence, or a buried shame, mark XP.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you has heard me name something that should not be named?
- Which of you saw my hands shake after I spoke that Name?
- Which of you thinks naming the darkness only calls it closer?
- Which of you trusts me to know when silence is safer?

---

**Wandering Songsmith**

You walk the roads between kaers carrying news, songs, and the comfort of shared voice. When you arrive in a settlement and perform for the people, take advantage on your next attempt to get information, passage, or shelter.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you first heard me sing by a roadside fire?
- Which of you knows the song I refuse to finish?
- Which of you has traveled the farthest with me already?
- Which of you thinks my wandering is running from something?

## Starting Moves

**Weave Tale**
When you tell a tale of a hero, a place, or a deed to strengthen its pattern, roll +CHA. On a 10+, choose 2. On a 7-9, choose 1.
- One listener is inspired; they take +1 forward on their next dangerous act.
- The tale reveals a hidden truth: the GM shares a useful detail about the subject.
- The pattern holds: the Horror mark, corruption, or despair touching the subject weakens visibly.

**Name the Nameless**
When you speak the true Name of a creature, place, or Horror-taint to strip its power, roll +CHA. On a 10+, it flinches, falters, or is revealed for what it truly is; take +1 forward against it. On a 7-9, your naming wounds it, but the effort costs you: the GM tells you what it learned about you in return.

**Thread Sight**
When you study a person, object, or place for the magical threads that bind it to the world, roll +WIS. On a 10+, ask the GM 2 of the following. On a 7-9, ask 1.
- What thread connects this to something greater?
- What thread here has been severed or corrupted?
- What Name does this answer to?

**Lore of the Scourge**
You carry the histories that survived the Long Night. When you consult your knowledge about a kaer, a Horror, a Namegiver culture, or the world before the Scourge, roll +INT. On a 10+, the GM tells you something specific and useful. On a 7-9, the GM tells you something general, and hints at what lore has been lost or twisted.

**Heartening Word**
When you speak courage into an ally who is afraid, wounded, or despairing, they heal 1d4 HP or shake off a fear or compulsion. You can only do this for each ally once per scene.

**Performance of Naming**
When all attention is on your performance and you invoke the Names of those present, each ally in earshot gains +1 armor against Horror powers, magical fear, and corruption until the performance ends or you are silenced.

## Advances

- **Voice of the Passion:** Choose a Passion (Astendar, Floranuus, Chorrolis, etc.). When you invoke its ideals in a tale or naming, your Weave Tale choices also grant +1 ongoing for that scene to anyone who shares that Passion's values.
- **Unraveler:** When you Name a Horror or corruption and roll a 12+, it recoils entirely, losing its hold on one victim, place, or object.
- **Living Library:** Once per session, declare that you know an obscure tale about the exact situation at hand. The GM must incorporate it.
- **Thread Singer:** When you Weave Tale for a magical item, you may awaken or strengthen one thread; the GM tells you what it can now do.
- **Tale of Shields:** When you perform a tale of endurance during a rest, each ally who listens heals an additional 1d6 HP.
- **Improved Stat:** Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +2.
- **Superior Stat** *(Requires: Improved Stat, at least one other advance)*: Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +3.

---

## Drives

- Preserve a story, Name, or tradition that would otherwise be lost.
- Name a Horror, a corruption, or a hidden truth others are afraid to speak.
- Inspire someone to act with courage through your words or art.
- Discover and record something new about the world after the Scourge.
