## Background

Pick one.

---

**Kaer Opener**

You were among the first to unseal your kaer and step outside after the Scourge. When you enter a new ruin, settlement, or wild place for the first time, ask the GM one: what has changed since the Scourge, what still works as it was meant to, or what left its mark here?

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you was in the kaer when I opened the door?
- Which of you followed me out into the light?
- Which of you does not understand what those first steps cost?
- Which of you saw what I saw outside and chose to go back in?

---

**Horror-Mark Reader**

You have learned to read the signs Horrors leave on the land: twisted growth, maddened animals, warped stone. When you study a trail or area for signs of Horror passage, the GM tells you how recent, how powerful, and in which direction.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you have I pulled back from a Horror-marked path?
- Which of you thinks I see danger where there is none?
- Which of you carries something I suspect bears a Horror-mark?
- Which of you has seen me freeze when the signs were too fresh?

---

**Wilderness Thread Weaver**

You weave threads to the living land itself -- reading weather in root patterns and danger in birdsong. When you spend a few minutes attuning to a natural place, take +1 forward to navigate, forage, or avoid danger there.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you has trusted my reading of the land and been proven right?
- Which of you thinks thread magic belongs only in cities and libraries?
- Which of you walked with me when I followed a thread to something unexpected?
- Which of you has seen me speak to the land and heard it answer?

---

**Expedition Pathfinder**

You have guided groups through post-Scourge wilderness before -- not all of them came back whole. When you lead a group through dangerous terrain, you always spot the first danger before it spots you.

When you introduce yourself to the other PCs, ask one or more of the following:
- Which of you survived an expedition I led?
- Which of you blames me for someone who did not come back?
- Which of you hired me despite my reputation?
- Which of you has seen me turn back from a path and refuse to explain why?

## Starting Moves

**Read the Corruption**
When you study a creature, place, or object for signs of Horror-taint, roll +WIS. On a 10+, the GM tells you clearly: is it tainted, how deeply, and what kind of Horror left the mark. On a 7-9, you get a strong impression -- tainted or clean -- but the details are unclear or unsettling.

**Silent Passage**
When you move through dangerous terrain without being detected, roll +DEX. On a 10+, you pass unseen and unheard -- choose one bonus: you find a safe path others can follow, you spot something hidden, or you get close enough to observe without risk. On a 7-9, you pass through, but choose one cost: you leave a trail, something senses you but cannot find you, or the safe window is brief.

**Scouring Eye**
When you survey an area from a vantage point or spend time studying a ruin, ask the GM two of the following: Where is the danger? What is the safest way through? What was this place before the Scourge? Is there something valuable or useful here? What has been here recently?

**Kaer Sense**
When you enter a sealed or partially opened kaer, ancient ruin, or underground shelter, roll +WIS. On a 10+, your instincts are sharp -- the GM tells you whether it is safe, occupied, or compromised by Horror influence. On a 7-9, you get a feeling -- something is wrong, or something is waiting -- but not what.

**Pathfinder's Strike**
When you attack from ambush or from a position your target did not know you occupied, deal your damage and choose one: you remain hidden, you pin them in place, or you create an opening for an ally.

**Live Off the Land**
When you forage, hunt, or find shelter in the post-Scourge wilderness, roll +WIS. On a 10+, you find what you need -- food, water, shelter, or medicinal herbs -- enough for the group. On a 7-9, you find enough for yourself and one other, or enough for all but it takes longer than expected.

## Advances

- **Horror-Scarred Veteran:** You have faced a Horror directly and survived. You are immune to mundane fear, and when you face Horror-spawned terror, take +1 to resist.
- **Thread Sight:** When you focus on a magical thread -- yours or another's -- you can trace it to its anchor. The GM tells you the direction and approximate distance.
- **Vanish:** When you are in natural terrain, you can simply disappear. No roll needed. Finding you requires magic or superhuman senses.
- **Dead Reckoning:** You never get lost. Even underground, even in astral fog, even in Horror-twisted terrain, you always know which direction leads to safety.
- **Coordinated Ambush:** When you set up an ambush and your allies follow your signal, everyone in the ambush takes +1 forward on their first action.
- **Improved Stat:** Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +2.
- **Superior Stat** *(Requires: Improved Stat, at least one other advance)*: Increase one of your stats by +1, to a max of +3.

## Drives

- Be the first to enter a dangerous place so others do not have to.
- Discover a lost kaer, ruin, or settlement and learn what happened there.
- Identify Horror corruption before it spreads.
- Guide someone safely through the changed world.
